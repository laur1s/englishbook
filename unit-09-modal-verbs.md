## Part 1: Essential Vocabulary / Pagrindinis žodynas

### Advice and Recommendations (Patarimai ir rekomendacijos)
- You should eat healthy food. - Turėtum valgyti sveiką maistą.
- You shouldn't smoke. - Neturėtum rūkyti.
- You should exercise regularly. - Turėtum sportuoti reguliariai.
- You should see a doctor. - Turėtum kreiptis į gydytoją.
- You should study harder. - Turėtum mokytis daugiau.

### Possibility (Galimybė)
- It may rain tomorrow. - Rytoj gali lyti.
- It might be cold. - Gali būti šalta.
- She may come late. - Ji gali pavėluoti.
- He might call you. - Jis gali tau paskambinti.

### Deductions and Certainty (Išvados ir tikrumas)
- You must be tired. - Tu tikriausiai pavargęs.
- She can't be at home. - Ji negali būti namuose.
- It must be difficult. - Tai tikriausiai sunku.
- They can't be serious. - Jie negali kalbėti rimtai.

### Obligation and Necessity (Pareiga ir būtinybė)
- You must wear a seatbelt. - Turi užsisegti saugos diržą.
- Students must do homework. - Studentai turi daryti namų darbus.
- You mustn't park here. - Čia negalima statyti automobilio.

## Part 2: Grammar - Modal Verbs for Deduction / Gramatika - Modaliniai veiksmažodžiai išvadoms

### Must for Certainty (Tikrumas)
**Structure:** subject + must + base verb

- Used when you are sure something is true based on evidence
- Must is followed by the base form (infinitive without "to")

**Examples:**
- The lights are off. She must be asleep. (I'm sure)
- He has a new car. He must be rich. (Logical conclusion)
- You're tired. You must go to bed. (Strong advice)

### Can't for Impossibility (Neįmanomumas)
**Structure:** subject + can't + base verb

- Used when you are sure something is NOT true
- Can't = cannot

**Examples:**
- The lights are on. She can't be asleep. (Impossible)
- He's only 5 years old. He can't drive a car. (Impossible)
- You're not hungry. You can't be hungry after that big meal. (Impossible)

**Must vs Can't:**
- The phone is ringing. He must be at home. (certain)
- The phone is ringing. He can't be at home. (impossible)

---

## Part 3: Grammar - Modal Verbs for Possibility / Gramatika - Modaliniai veiksmažodžiai galimybei

### May and Might (Galbūt)
**Structure:** subject + may/might + base verb

- Used to talk about future possibility
- May and might have the same meaning
- Might is slightly less certain than may
- No difference in past or negative forms

**Examples:**
- It may rain tomorrow. (50% chance)
- It might rain tomorrow. (same meaning)
- She may come to the party. (possible)
- He might call later. (possible)

### May vs Might in Questions
- May I...? (polite request) - Ar galiu...?
- Might I...? (very formal)

**Examples:**
- May I use your phone? (polite)
- Might I ask you a question? (very formal)

### Negative Forms
- may not / might not = might not happen
- cannot = impossible

**Examples:**
- It may not rain. (possible that it won't rain)
- She might not come. (possible she won't come)
- It cannot rain. (impossible)

---

## Part 4: Grammar - Modal Verbs for Advice / Gramatika - Modaliniai veiksmažodžiai patarimams

### Should for Advice (Patarimai)
**Structure:** subject + should + base verb

- Used to give advice or recommendations
- Should = it's a good idea
- Shouldn't = it's not a good idea

**Examples:**
- You should eat more vegetables. (good advice)
- You shouldn't eat too much junk food. (bad idea)
- Students should study every day. (recommendation)

### Should vs Must
- Should = good idea (but not obligatory)
- Must = necessary/required

**Examples:**
- You should wear warm clothes. (good idea)
- You must wear a seatbelt. (law/requirement)

### Questions with Should
- Should I...? (asking for advice)
- What should I do?

**Examples:**
- Should I call him? (asking for advice)
- What should I wear to the party?

## Exercise 8: Extended Vocabulary / Pratimas 8: Išplėstas žodynas

### Modal Verbs and Their Uses (Modaliniai veiksmažodžiai ir jų naudojimas)

- Must - Obligation/strong advice - Turi/privalai
- Should - Advice - Turėtum
- May/Might - Possibility - Gali
- Can - Ability/permission - Gali
- Could - Past ability/polite request - Galėjai/galėčiau
- Mustn't - Prohibition - Negali
- Can't - Impossibility - Negali

### Situations for Modals (Situacijos modalams)

- Health advice - Sveikatos patarimai
- Travel permissions - Kelionių leidimai
- Study obligations - Studijų įsipareigojimai
- Weather possibilities - Oro galimybės
- Polite requests - Mandagūs prašymai

## Additional Cultural Notes / Papildomos kultūrinės pastabos

- **Politeness:** English uses "could" and "may" for polite requests, unlike Lithuanian directness.
- **Advice Giving:** In English-speaking cultures, advice is often given indirectly with "should" rather than commands.
- **Prohibition:** "Mustn't" is stronger than "shouldn't" for rules.

---

## Useful Phrases / Naudingi posakiai

- You should... - Turėtum...
- You shouldn't... - Neturėtum...
- It may/might... - Gali...
- You must... - Turi...
- You can't... - Negali...
- What should I do? - Ką man daryti?
- Do you think I should...? - Manai, kad turėčiau...?
- I'm not sure. - Nesu tikras.
- That's a good idea. - Tai gera idėja.

---

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

- English speakers use "should" frequently to give polite advice
- "May I...?" is more polite than "Can I...?" for requests
- In English, we often use modals to be indirect when giving advice
- Lithuanians might be more direct; English speakers often soften advice with "should"
