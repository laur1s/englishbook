# Unit 23: People, Places, and Relative Clauses

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Add identifying information with **who, which/that, where**.
- Describe personality and places respectfully.
- Recommend a person, place, or thing with reasons.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Start with two short ideas, join them with the correct relative word, and then use the description to make a useful recommendation. Your listener should know **what it is, which one you mean, and why you recommend it**.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **who** for people, **which/that** for things, and **where** for places: *A guide is someone who helps visitors. A hostel is a place where travellers can stay. An adapter is a thing that connects devices.*

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> <strong>Who</strong> sieja informaciją apie žmogų, <strong>which/that</strong> – apie daiktą, <strong>where</strong> – apie vietą. Šie žodžiai padeda sujungti du trumpus sakinius į vieną aiškų apibūdinimą.</p>

### Join, do not repeat / Sujunkite nekartodami

| First idea | Second idea | Joined sentence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dalia is a guide. | She knows local history. | Dalia is a guide **who knows local history**. |
| I use an app. | It works offline. | I use an app **that works offline**. |
| This is the park. | We met there. | This is the park **where we met**. |

The relative clause normally comes directly after the person, thing, or place it describes.

**Common error:** do not repeat the subject: **a guide who speaks English**, not *a guide who she speaks English*.

### Recommendation language / Rekomendavimo frazės

**I recommend... because... / It is ideal for... / One advantage is... / However,...**

### Definition strategy / Apibrėžimo strategija

If you do not know a word, describe it:

- **It's a person who...**
- **It's a thing that you use to...**
- **It's a place where...**

This is a real communication skill, not a failure. Keep speaking and invite a guess.

### Useful, Respectful Descriptions / Naudingi ir pagarbūs apibūdinimai

Describe qualities that are relevant to the situation:

| Quality | Meaning in context | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| reliable | does what they promise | *a reliable colleague who arrives on time* |
| patient | stays calm while waiting or helping | *a patient tutor who explains things again* |
| welcoming | makes visitors feel comfortable | *a welcoming host who introduces everyone* |
| practical | focuses on useful solutions | *practical advice that is easy to follow* |
| peaceful | quiet and relaxing | *a peaceful park where people can rest* |
| accessible | easy to enter or use for different people | *a museum that has a lift and seating* |

Avoid unnecessary comments about age, body, nationality, health, or other personal features. Say what makes the person or place suitable: *I recommend Greta because she explains prices clearly*, not only *Greta is nice*.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> rekomenduodami pateikite konkretų, su situacija susijusį požymį. <em>A café which is good</em> yra neaišku; <em>a café that has vegetarian options and quiet tables</em> suteikia naudingos informacijos.</p>

### Building Defining Relative Clauses / Tikslinamųjų sakinių sudarymas

A defining relative clause tells us exactly which person, thing, or place we mean. It has no comma in these A2 examples:

- *The nurse **who speaks Lithuanian** works on Mondays.*
- *The bus **that goes to the airport** leaves here.*
- *This is the room **where we have meetings**.*

Use **who** as the subject for a person and **that/which** for a thing. Use **where** when the meaning is *in/at that place*. Compare:

- *This is the library **that opens late**.* (**library** performs the action)
- *This is the library **where I study**.* (I study **in** the library)

At this level, keep the relative word in the sentence. This makes your descriptions clear and reduces avoidable errors.

## Exercise 4: Read Three Recommendations / Pratimas 4

Read once for the three names. Read again and underline the relative clause that explains each recommendation.

> Green Table is a café that serves simple local food. Dalia is a guide who tells clear and lively stories about local history. River Park is a place where children can play safely and adults can walk by the water.

1. Which place serves local food?
2. Who is Dalia?
3. Why might families choose River Park?
4. Which recommendation is best for a history lover?

## Exercise 5: Guess the Word / Pratimas 5

Write definitions with relative clauses for **dentist, umbrella, station, password**. A partner guesses each word.

Model: **It is a person who checks your teeth.** If you study alone, wait ten minutes, shuffle your definitions, and guess the words without looking at the list.

## Exercise 6: Write a Recommendation / Pratimas 6

Recommend one person, place, and useful object. Model: **My neighbour Tomas is someone who always helps people. The old library is a place where I can study quietly. I also recommend the bus app that shows live departure times.**

Write 8–10 sentences. Give a reason and one useful detail for each recommendation.

**Sentence starters:**
**I recommend... who... / A place that I often recommend is... / It is somewhere where... / A useful thing is... that... / This is a good choice for... because...**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] I used **who**, **that/which**, and **where** accurately.
- [ ] Each relative clause follows the noun it describes.
- [ ] I gave reasons, not only positive adjectives.
- [ ] My descriptions are specific and respectful.
- [ ] A visitor could act on at least one recommendation.

### Sixty-second recommendation / Vienos minutės rekomendacija

Choose your strongest recommendation. Speak for one minute using only five notes: **name – category – description – reason – practical detail**. End with **I recommend it to people who...**

For each recommendation, add the audience and a reason: *For a visitor who enjoys history, I recommend...* Check that the detail is relevant and respectful.

## Exercise 7: Read a Neighbourhood Guide / Pratimas 7

> **Three useful places near the station**
> Start at Saulė Information Centre, which is opposite platform 2. Lina is the adviser who usually works there in the morning. She gives clear transport information and can mark an accessible route on a map. For lunch, try Garden Spoon, a small café that serves soup, salads, and two vegetarian dishes every day. It is a good choice for people who need a quiet table. Finally, Oak House is the library where visitors can use Wi-Fi without paying. The ground floor has a lift, but the reading room that stays open until 8 p.m. is upstairs.

1. Where is Saulė Information Centre?
2. Who can mark an accessible route?
3. What kind of food does Garden Spoon serve?
4. Who may especially like the café?
5. What can visitors use free at Oak House?
6. Which room stays open until 8 p.m.?

## Exercise 8: Who, That, or Where? / Pratimas 8

Complete each sentence. Use **who**, **that**, or **where**.

1. I need a hairdresser _____ can work on Saturday.
2. Is there a shop _____ sells phone chargers nearby?
3. This is the square _____ the weekend market takes place.
4. Tomas is the neighbour _____ looks after our cat.
5. We chose a hotel _____ has family rooms.
6. The community centre is a place _____ new residents can meet people.
7. She uses an app _____ reads text aloud.
8. I know a mechanic _____ explains the repair costs clearly.

## Exercise 9: Make a Useful Local Recommendation / Pratimas 9

Write an 80–100-word recommendation for a new resident or visitor. Include:

- one person **who** can help;
- one place **where** the reader can do something;
- one service or object **that** is useful;
- two practical details such as price, opening time, transport, or accessibility;
- a reason with **because**.

**Model opening:** *For a visitor who wants to explore the city by bus, I recommend the travel centre beside the station. It is a place where you can buy a day ticket and get a free map...*

Self-check: Can a reader act on your recommendation? Are all descriptions specific, relevant, and respectful?

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can give a clear recommendation and reason.
- [ ] I can describe an unknown word instead of stopping.
- [ ] I can choose between **that** and **where** by checking the meaning.
- [ ] I can describe people respectfully and include practical access information.
