# Unit 24: A2 Communication Project

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Combine the language from all 24 units in realistic tasks.
- Read, plan, negotiate, write, and self-correct.
- Show A2 ability through evidence, not one perfect answer.

### How to use this project / Kaip atlikti projektą

Complete the project in four short rounds:

1. **Understand:** list the guest's needs and the limits.
2. **Decide:** compare options and calculate the cost.
3. **Communicate:** create the schedule, message, and spoken explanation.
4. **Repair:** respond to a change and improve unclear language.

Do not try to write the final message immediately. Keep your notes, first draft, corrections, and final version: together they show what you can do.

## Project Brief / Projekto aprašas

Plan a two-day visit for an English-speaking guest. Your guest needs transport, food, one activity, a place to stay, and a backup plan. Keep the language clear and practical.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Pirmiausia pasižymėkite šešis apribojimus, tada pasirinkite variantus pagal kainas. Patikrinkite laikų seką, biudžetą, vieną sąlygą su <strong>if</strong>, mandagų klausimą ir atsarginį planą. Tikslas – aiški, reali komunikacija, ne tobulas tekstas.</p>

### Your A2 Communication Toolkit / Tavo A2 bendravimo įrankiai

This project brings together practical language from the course:

| Communication job | Useful language |
| --- | --- |
| introduce and welcome | *Hi Maya, it's lovely to meet you.* |
| state a routine or fact | *The museum opens at ten.* |
| describe an arrangement | *We're meeting at the station.* |
| explain a past change | *The café closed last month.* |
| discuss experience | *Have you ever tried Lithuanian beetroot soup?* |
| compare | *The bus is cheaper, but the taxi is more convenient.* |
| explain necessity | *You need to keep your ticket.* |
| give an option | *We could visit the museum instead.* |
| predict a result | *If it rains, we'll take the bus.* |
| repair communication | *Sorry, could you say that again? Do you mean...?* |

You do not need to use every grammar form in every sentence. Good A2 communication is understandable, relevant, connected, and responsive.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Vertinimo principas:</strong> vieno „tobulo“ maršruto nėra. Sprendimas yra tinkamas, jei atitinka Mayos poreikius, telpa į biudžetą, aiškiai paaiškina pasirinkimus ir turi realų atsarginį variantą.</p>

## Exercise 1: Select Key Information / Pratimas 1

Guest profile: **Maya arrives Friday at 18:20. She is vegetarian, enjoys nature and local history, has a €90 weekend budget, and cannot walk long distances. Heavy rain is possible on Saturday afternoon.**

List the six planning constraints. Treat **nature and local history** as one interests category.

Use this weekend price guide:

| Choice | Price |
| --- | ---: |
| Bus from the station | €3 |
| Taxi from the station | €18 |
| Hostel | €32 |
| Hotel | €55 |
| Market | Free |
| Castle | €12 |
| Park | Free |
| Museum | €10 |
| Vegetarian food allowance | €25 |

## Exercise 2: Choose and Justify / Pratimas 2

Choose one from each pair and give a reason: **bus/taxi from the station; hostel/hotel; market/castle visit; park/museum in rain**. Keep the total at or below €90, including the food allowance. Use a comparison, **because**, and **too/enough**.

One acceptable decision may differ from another. Your choice succeeds when the calculation is correct, it respects Maya's needs, and the reason matches the information.

Show the calculation. Remember that a rain option replaces the outdoor activity; do not add the price twice unless your plan actually includes both activities. Explain one trade-off: *The taxi is more comfortable, but it is too expensive for this budget.*

## Exercise 3: Build the Schedule / Pratimas 3

Put these functions into a logical two-day order: **arrival; check-in; dinner; Saturday activity; lunch; backup activity; departure**. Add realistic times and transport.

Check travel time between activities. Avoid an activity that conflicts with arrival, meals, accessibility needs, or the weather information.

Include at least 30 minutes between the station arrival and dinner. Maya cannot walk long distances, so name transport or state that two places are close together. Mark the backup as conditional, not as an extra activity at the same time.

## Exercise 4: Read and Repair the Plan / Pratimas 4

> Maya arrive at 18:20 and I meet her yesterday at the station. We going to take a taxi because the hostel is enough far. On Saturday, if it will rain, we visit the history museum. She don't have to eat meat because I booked a vegetarian café who serves local dishes.

Find and correct all the errors.

## Exercise 5: Handle a Change / Pratimas 5

At 12:00 on Saturday, the activity is cancelled and the guest's phone battery is almost empty. Write a four-line dialogue that explains the problem, suggests two options, checks preference, and confirms a decision.

**Model structure:**

> A: The park tour has been cancelled, and your phone battery is low.
> B: Oh no. What can we do?
> A: We could visit the museum or have lunch first. Would you prefer the museum?
> B: Yes, let's go there. We'll charge my phone at the café afterwards.

Your details may differ, but all four communication functions must be present.

Do not assume that Maya can receive a long online message. Include a practical communication solution such as writing down the address, using your phone, asking at an information desk, or agreeing on a meeting point.

## Exercise 6: Write the Final Message / Pratimas 6

Write 100–130 words to Maya. Include arrival instructions, arrangements, comparisons, one condition, one rule or necessity, and a polite question. Model opening: **Hi Maya, I'm looking forward to seeing you on Friday. I'll meet you at the station at 18:20. The bus is cheaper than a taxi, but...**

**Paragraph plan:**

1. welcome + arrival and transport;
2. accommodation + Friday food;
3. Saturday activity + reason;
4. weather condition + backup;
5. practical rule or need + polite question.

**Self-edit in three passes:** first check whether all information is present; next check verbs and sentence links; finally check spelling, capitals, times, and prices.

## Exercise 7: Speaking Rehearsal / Pratimas 7

Present the plan for 90 seconds. Then respond to two changes: bad weather and a closed restaurant. Sentence starters: **Our first plan is... / If that changes... / We could... / Would you prefer...?**

Use six keywords instead of reading the final message. Record two attempts. In the second attempt, aim for clearer organisation and one successful self-correction: **Sorry, I mean...**

## Exercise 8: Final Self-Check / Pratimas 8

Give evidence for each statement: I can describe routine and current action; narrate a past event; discuss experience; plan a future action; compare choices; give advice; explain a rule; solve a problem; connect sentences.

For each claim, quote or point to one sentence from your project. If this project does not demonstrate a skill such as narrating a past event, write one short extra example. A checked box without evidence is not yet a useful assessment.

## Exercise 9: Read the Final Update / Pratimas 9

> **Friday, 15:40**
> Hi! My train has been delayed by 35 minutes, so I will arrive at 18:55, not 18:20. My phone battery is at 12%. I have eaten already, so I only need a light snack this evening. I checked the weather: Saturday morning should be dry, but heavy rain will probably start at 13:00. Could we do the outdoor activity before lunch? Please send one short message with the new meeting place. Maya

1. What is Maya's new arrival time?
2. How long is the delay?
3. Why should the reply be short?
4. Which original evening need has changed?
5. When is heavy rain likely to start?
6. What change does Maya request for Saturday?

## Exercise 10: Revise the Plan / Pratimas 10

Update three parts of your work after reading Maya's message:

1. Write a message of no more than **35 words** with the new meeting place and one clear identifying detail.
2. Move the outdoor activity to a realistic time and show where lunch fits.
3. Recalculate the budget only if your choices have changed.

**Model short message:** *Thanks for the update. I'll meet you at 18:55 inside the station, beside the information desk. I'm wearing a green jacket. Please save your battery. See you soon!* (31 words)

## Exercise 11: Final A2 Portfolio / Pratimas 11

Submit or present four pieces of evidence:

- **Plan:** a timetable and budget with a labelled backup.
- **Message:** the 100–130-word message from Exercise 6, revised after feedback.
- **Interaction:** the problem-solving dialogue from Exercise 5 or a recording of it.
- **Reflection:** 60–80 words about one successful strategy and one next step.

Reflection model: *My plan is easy to follow because I used times and sequence words. I also gave Maya two choices when the activity was cancelled. I sometimes forgot the word after “if”, so I checked every conditional. Next, I want to practise speaking without reading every sentence.*

Ask a partner or teacher to respond to meaning first: Is the plan workable? Is any information missing? Then correct up to three language points that most affect clarity.

## Exercise 12: Capstone Conference / Pratimas 12

Speak for 2–3 minutes, then answer three questions. The listener chooses one question from each group:

- **Reason:** Why did you choose this accommodation or transport?
- **Change:** What will you do if the weather or opening time changes?
- **Clarification:** What do you mean by...? / Could you repeat the meeting place?

Useful repair phrases: *Let me explain that again. / I mean... / Sorry, I gave the wrong time. / The correct time is... / Have I answered your question?*

This is not a memory test. You may use your timetable and price notes, but try not to read the full message aloud.

## Success Checklist / Sėkmės kriterijai

- [ ] The plan is understandable, realistic, and within budget.
- [ ] The message uses connected sentences and at least five A2 structures.
- [ ] The speaking response includes repair language and a backup option.
- [ ] Open work is self-checked, not automatically graded.
- [ ] Every stated guest need is visible in the plan.
- [ ] The total cost is shown and does not exceed €90.
- [ ] The revised plan responds accurately to Maya's update.
- [ ] The final portfolio contains clear evidence of reading, writing, speaking, and interaction.
- [ ] Feedback focuses on meaning and the most useful next steps, not perfection.

### Reflection and next step / Refleksija ir kitas žingsnis

Complete these sentences after checking your work:

- **The clearest part of my plan is... because...**
- **I corrected...**
- **I still pause when I need to...**
- **In my next three study sessions, I will practise...**

This project is evidence of current ability and a guide for what to practise next; it is not a claim that every A2 skill is permanently complete.
