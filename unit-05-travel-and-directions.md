## Part 2: Directions (Kryptys)

### Basic Directions (Pagrindinės kryptys)
- left - kairėje
- right - dešinėje
- straight on/ahead - tiesiai
- back - atgal
- up - aukštyn
- down - žemyn

### Giving Directions (Krypčių nurodymas)
- Go straight on - Eikite tiesiai
- Turn left - Sukite kairėn
- Turn right - Sukite dešinėn
- Go past... - Eikite pro...
- Cross the street - Pereikite gatvę
- Take the first/second left - Sukite pirmąja/antrąja į kairę
- It's on your left/right - Tai yra jūsų kairėje/dešinėje
- It's opposite... - Tai yra priešais...
- It's next to... - Tai yra šalia...
- It's between... - Tai yra tarp...
- It's near... - Tai yra netoli...
- It's far from... - Tai yra toli nuo...

### Asking for Directions (Kaip paklausti kelio)
- Excuse me, where is...? - Atsiprašau, kur yra...?
- How do I get to...? - Kaip nuvykti į...?
- Is it far? - Ar toli?
- Can you help me? - Ar galite man padėti?
- I'm lost - Aš pasiklydau
- Which way to...? - Kokiu keliu į...?

| Preposition | Lithuanian | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| in | viduje | in the box |
| on | ant | on the table |
| at | prie | at the bus stop |
| next to | šalia | next to the bank |
| near | netoli | near the hotel |
| between | tarp | between the shops |
| opposite | priešais | opposite the church |
| in front of | priešais | in front of the cinema |
| behind | už | behind the building |
| under | po | under the bridge |
| over/above | virš | over the door |

**Examples:**
- The bank is **next to** the post office.
- The café is **opposite** the museum.
- The hotel is **between** the restaurant and the shop.
- The park is **behind** the church.

## Part 4: There is / There are

### Formation (Sudarymas)

**There is** - singular (vienaskaita)
- **There is** a bank on this street. - Šioje gatvėje yra bankas.
- **There is** a cat in the garden. - Sode yra katė.

**There are** - plural (daugiskaita)
- **There are** two hotels in the city. - Mieste yra du viešbučiai.
- **There are** many shops here. - Čia yra daug parduotuvių.

### Negative (Neigiamasis)
- **There isn't** (is not) a hospital near here.
- **There aren't** (are not) any buses on Sunday.

### Questions (Klausimai)
- **Is there** a bank near here? - Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.
- **Are there** any shops? - Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.

## Useful Phrases / Naudingi posakiai

**Asking for help:**
- Can you help me? - Ar galite man padėti?
- I'm looking for... - Aš ieškau...
- I need to get to... - Man reikia nuvykti į...
- Could you show me on the map? - Ar galėtumėte parodyti žemėlapyje?

**Traveling:**
- What time does the train leave? - Kada išvyksta traukinys?
- Which platform? - Kuri platforma?
- A ticket to..., please - Bilietą į..., prašau
- Single or return? - Į vieną pusę ar į abi puses?
- How much is a ticket? - Kiek kainuoja bilietas?

**At the hotel:**
- I have a reservation - Turiu rezervaciją
- Check in / Check out - Registracija / Išsiregistravimas
- Can I have the key? - Ar galiu gauti raktą?
- What time is breakfast? - Kada pusryčiai?

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

**In English-speaking countries:**
- People usually give distances in miles/yards (US/UK) or meters (other English-speaking countries)
- Be polite when asking for directions: "Excuse me" or "Sorry"
- Say "thank you" when someone helps you
- If you're lost, don't be afraid to ask - people usually help tourists

**Useful distances:**
- Close/near - less than 10 minutes walk
- Not far - 10-20 minutes
- Quite far - more than 20 minutes
- Very far - need transport

---

## Tips for Lithuanian Speakers / Patarimai lietuviams

⚠️ **Common mistakes:**
1. Don't confuse "in" (inside), "on" (surface), and "at" (point/place)
2. Remember: "There is" + singular, "There are" + plural
3. Use "on" for streets: "on Main Street" (not "in Main Street")
4. Say "on foot" (not "by foot")

**Lithuanian vs English:**
- Lithuanian has more case endings for locations
- English relies more on prepositions
- Be careful with "at", "on", and "in" - they're different from Lithuanian cases
