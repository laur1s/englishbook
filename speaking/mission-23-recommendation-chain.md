## Useful Building Blocks / Naudingi sakiniai

- I recommend someone who... — Rekomenduoju žmogų, kuris...
- ...is a person who... — ...yra žmogus, kuris...
- ...is a place where... — ...yra vieta, kur...
- ...is an app that... — ...yra programėlė, kuri...
- It is useful for people who... — Tai naudinga žmonėms, kurie...
- I recommend it because... — Rekomenduoju, nes...
- For example,... — Pavyzdžiui,...

## Success Check / Sėkmės kriterijai

Specific details make a recommendation more useful than general praise. Instead of saying a place is simply “nice,” explain what a visitor can do there or why it suits a particular person.

Your talk succeeds when it recommends one person, one place, and one thing; uses the correct relative word for each; and gives three different, concrete reasons. On the second attempt, recommend all three to a different listener—such as a family, student, or visitor—and adapt the reasons to that person.
