## Part 2: Grammar - Present Simple / Gramatika

### Formation (Sudarymas)

**Affirmative (Teigiamasis)**

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I/You/We/They | work | I work every day |
| He/She/It | works | She works in an office |

⚠️ **Important:** Add -s/-es to the verb for he/she/it!

### Spelling Rules (Rašybos taisyklės)

- Most verbs: add -s → work**s**, play**s**, eat**s**
- Verbs ending in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es → watch**es**, go**es**, do**es**
- Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -es → study → stud**ies**, cry → cr**ies**

### Negative (Neigiamasis)

| Subject | Auxiliary | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I/You/We/They | don't + verb | I don't work on Sundays |
| He/She/It | doesn't + verb | He doesn't like coffee |

### Questions (Klausimai)

| Auxiliary | Subject | Verb | Example |
|-----------|---------|------|---------|
| Do | I/you/we/they | work? | Do you work here? |
| Does | he/she/it | work? | Does she work here? |

## Part 3: Adverbs of Frequency / Dažnumo prieveiksmiai

| English | Lithuanian | Frequency % |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| always | visada | 100% |
| usually | paprastai | 80% |
| often | dažnai | 60% |
| sometimes | kartais | 40% |
| rarely / seldom | retai | 20% |
| never | niekada | 0% |

**Position:** Usually before the main verb, but after "to be"

- I **always** wake up early.
- She is **usually** happy.

## Useful Phrases / Naudingi posakiai

- What time do you...? - Kada tu...?
- How often do you...? - Kaip dažnai tu...?
- I usually... - Aš paprastai...
- On weekdays - Darbo dienomis
- At the weekend - Savaitgalį
- From Monday to Friday - Nuo pirmadienio iki penktadienio
- In my free time - Laisvu laiku
- Every morning/evening - Kiekvieną rytą/vakarą

---

## Tips for Lithuanian Speakers / Patarimai lietuviams

⚠️ **Common mistakes:**

1. Don't forget -s/-es for he/she/it: "She work" ✗ → "She works" ✓
2. Use auxiliary do/does in questions and negatives
3. Present Simple is for habits and routines, not happening now
4. Position of adverbs: before main verb, after "be"
