# Unit 8: Comparatives and Superlatives
## Skyrius 8: Lyginamieji ir aukščiausiojo laipsnio būdvardžiai

### Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai
- Form comparative and superlative adjectives / Formuoti lyginamuosius ir aukščiausiojo laipsnio būdvardžius
- Use comparative adjectives to compare two things / Naudoti lyginamuosius būdvardžius dviejų dalykų palyginimui
- Use superlative adjectives to compare more than two things / Naudoti aukščiausiojo laipsnio būdvardžius daugiau nei dviejų dalykų palyginimui
- Understand irregular comparative and superlative forms / Suprasti nereguliarias formas

## Part 2: Grammar - Comparative Adjectives / Gramatika - Lyginamieji būdvardžiai

### Formation Rules (Formavimo taisyklės)

#### Short Adjectives (1 syllable) - Add -er
- tall → taller
- big → bigger
- fast → faster
- hot → hotter

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is bigger than Kaunas.
- Summer is hotter than winter.

#### Most Longer Adjectives - Use "more"
- expensive → more expensive
- interesting → more interesting
- beautiful → more beautiful
- difficult → more difficult

Many adjectives with three or more syllables use **more**. Some two-syllable adjectives use **-er**, and some allow both forms, so learn common forms as vocabulary. Adjectives ending in **-y** follow the rule below.

**Examples:**
- A car is more expensive than a bicycle.
- English is more difficult than Lithuanian.

#### 2-syllable adjectives ending in -y - Change y to i, add -er
- happy → happier
- easy → easier
- lucky → luckier
- funny → funnier

**Examples:**
- She is happier now.
- This exercise is easier than the last one.

### Irregular Comparatives (Nereguliarūs lyginamieji)
- good → better
- bad → worse
- far → farther/further
- little → less
- much/many → more

**Examples:**
- This book is better than that one.
- The weather is worse today.
- He walks further than I do.

### Structure: Comparative + than
- Subject + be + comparative adjective + than + object

**Examples:**
- My house is bigger than yours.
- English is more difficult than Spanish.
- She is happier than her brother.

### Equality with as...as / Lygybė su as...as

- **as + base adjective + as** means two things are equal in that quality: *This task is as easy as the last one.*
- **not as + base adjective + as** means the first thing has less of that quality: *Vilnius is not as big as London.*
- Do not use a comparative form inside this structure: *as easy as* ✓, *as easier as* ✗.

## Part 3: Grammar - Superlative Adjectives / Gramatika - Aukščiausiojo laipsnio būdvardžiai

### Formation Rules (Formavimo taisyklės)

#### Short Adjectives - Add -est
- tall → the tallest
- big → the biggest
- fast → the fastest
- hot → the hottest

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is the biggest city in Lithuania.
- August is the hottest month.

#### Long Adjectives - Use "most"
- expensive → the most expensive
- interesting → the most interesting
- beautiful → the most beautiful
- difficult → the most difficult

**Examples:**
- This is the most expensive car in the shop.
- English is the most difficult subject.

#### 2-syllable adjectives ending in -y - Change y to i, add -est
- happy → the happiest
- easy → the easiest
- lucky → the luckiest

**Examples:**
- She is the happiest person I know.
- This is the easiest exercise.

### Irregular Superlatives (Nereguliarūs aukščiausiojo laipsnio)
- good → the best
- bad → the worst
- far → the farthest/furthest
- little → the least
- much/many → the most

**Examples:**
- This is the best book I've read.
- He is the worst student in class.
- This is the furthest I've ever walked.

### Structure: The + superlative
- Subject + be + the + superlative adjective + ...

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is the biggest city in Lithuania.
- This is the most beautiful place I've seen.
- She is the happiest person in the world.

Use **one of the + superlative + plural noun** when something belongs to a top group: *Vilnius is one of the oldest cities in the region.*

## Additional Cultural Notes / Papildomos kultūrinės pastabos

- **Politeness in Comparisons:** A direct negative comparison can sound critical. In a sensitive situation, give a reason or frame it as a preference: "I prefer this car because it uses less fuel."
- **Advertising:** Superlatives are common in ads: "The best," "The fastest," "The cheapest."
- **Sports Commentary:** Comparisons are frequent: "He runs faster than...," "This is the best team ever."

---

- How much more...? - Kiek daugiau...?

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

- English speakers use comparisons frequently in conversation
- Use **one of the + superlative + plural noun** for membership in a top group: "one of the best restaurants"
- Use **than** after a comparative when you name the second person or thing: "bigger than Kaunas"
- In English, we compare quality, not just size or quantity
