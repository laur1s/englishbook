## Part 2: Grammar - Comparative Adjectives / Gramatika - Lyginamieji būdvardžiai

### Formation Rules (Formavimo taisyklės)

#### Short Adjectives (1 syllable) - Add -er
- tall → taller
- big → bigger
- fast → faster
- hot → hotter

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is bigger than Kaunas.
- Summer is hotter than winter.

#### Long Adjectives (2+ syllables) - Use "more"
- expensive → more expensive
- interesting → more interesting
- beautiful → more beautiful
- difficult → more difficult

**Examples:**
- A car is more expensive than a bicycle.
- English is more difficult than Lithuanian.

#### 2-syllable adjectives ending in -y - Change y to i, add -er
- happy → happier
- easy → easier
- lucky → luckier
- funny → funnier

**Examples:**
- She is happier now.
- This exercise is easier than the last one.

### Irregular Comparatives (Nereguliarūs lyginamieji)
- good → better
- bad → worse
- far → farther/further
- little → less
- much/many → more

**Examples:**
- This book is better than that one.
- The weather is worse today.
- He walks further than I do.

### Structure: Comparative + than
- Subject + be + comparative adjective + than + object

**Examples:**
- My house is bigger than yours.
- English is more difficult than Spanish.
- She is happier than her brother.

## Part 3: Grammar - Superlative Adjectives / Gramatika - Aukščiausiojo laipsnio būdvardžiai

### Formation Rules (Formavimo taisyklės)

#### Short Adjectives - Add -est
- tall → the tallest
- big → the biggest
- fast → the fastest
- hot → the hottest

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is the biggest city in Lithuania.
- August is the hottest month.

#### Long Adjectives - Use "most"
- expensive → the most expensive
- interesting → the most interesting
- beautiful → the most beautiful
- difficult → the most difficult

**Examples:**
- This is the most expensive car in the shop.
- English is the most difficult subject.

#### 2-syllable adjectives ending in -y - Change y to i, add -est
- happy → the happiest
- easy → the easiest
- lucky → the luckiest

**Examples:**
- She is the happiest person I know.
- This is the easiest exercise.

### Irregular Superlatives (Nereguliarūs aukščiausiojo laipsnio)
- good → the best
- bad → the worst
- far → the farthest/furthest
- little → the least
- much/many → the most

**Examples:**
- This is the best book I've read.
- He is the worst student in class.
- This is the furthest I've ever walked.

### Structure: The + superlative
- Subject + be + the + superlative adjective + ...

**Examples:**
- Vilnius is the biggest city in Lithuania.
- This is the most beautiful place I've seen.
- She is the happiest person in the world.

## Additional Cultural Notes / Papildomos kultūrinės pastabos

- **Politeness in Comparisons:** English speakers avoid direct negative comparisons to be polite. Instead of "Your car is worse," say "My car is different."
- **Advertising:** Superlatives are common in ads: "The best," "The fastest," "The cheapest."
- **Sports Commentary:** Comparisons are frequent: "He runs faster than...," "This is the best team ever."

---

## Useful Phrases / Naudingi posakiai

- Which is bigger/better...? - Kuris yra didesnis/geresnis...?
- It's more expensive than... - Tai brangesnė nei...
- It's the most beautiful... - Tai gražiausia...
- Not as...as - Ne tokia...kaip
- Much more... - Daug daugiau...
- A bit more... - Šiek tiek daugiau...
- How much more...? - Kiek daugiau...?
- I think...is better - Manau, kad...yra geriau

---

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

- English speakers use comparisons frequently in conversation
- Superlatives often use "one of the" instead of "the" when there are several equal best things
- "Than" is always used with comparatives (unlike Lithuanian "už")
- In English, we compare quality, not just size or quantity
