# Unit 18: Work, Study, and Learning

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Describe responsibilities and learning goals.
- Use common **verb + to** and **verb + -ing** patterns.
- Ask for clarification in a class or workplace.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Learn each verb together with the form that follows it. Then read a practical course message, practise asking for clarification, and create a learning plan with a measurable next step.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Common verbs followed by **to + verb** include **want, need, hope, plan, decide**. Common verbs followed by **-ing** include **enjoy, finish, avoid, practise**. **Like/love/hate** can often use either form with little difference at this level.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Po <strong>want, need, hope, plan, decide</strong> dažniausiai vartojama <strong>to + veiksmažodis</strong>. Po <strong>enjoy, finish, avoid, practise</strong> vartojama <strong>-ing</strong> forma. Mokykitės visą junginį, ne vien atskirą veiksmažodį.</p>

### Learn the whole chunk / Mokykitės visą junginį

| **to + verb** | **verb + -ing** |
| --- | --- |
| want **to improve** | enjoy **working** |
| need **to practise** | finish **writing** |
| hope **to find** | avoid **checking** |
| plan **to apply** | practise **speaking** |
| decide **to start** | keep **learning** |

When a verb follows a preposition, use **-ing**: **good at organising, interested in learning, before sending**.

### Make a goal measurable / Konkretus tikslas

Compare:

- Vague: **I want to improve my English.**
- Useful: **I plan to practise speaking for ten minutes on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.**

A practical goal says **what, when, how often,** and **how you will check progress**.

### Clarification toolkit / Pasitikslinimo frazės

- **Sorry, could you explain that again?**
- **What does ... mean?**
- **Do you mean that I should...?**
- **Could you show me an example?**
- **So, first I..., and then I..., right?**
- **When do I need to finish it?**

Use a polite opening, name the exact unclear point, and check your understanding.

### Work and Learning Chunks / Darbo ir mokymosi junginiai

| Chunk | Example | Lithuanian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| apply for a job / course | I plan to apply for the course. | kandidatuoti į darbą / teikti paraišką į kursą |
| attend a class / meeting | She attends a class twice a week. | lankyti pamoką / dalyvauti susitikime |
| meet a deadline | We need to finish by Friday. | atlikti iki termino |
| gain experience | He wants to gain office experience. | įgyti patirties |
| improve a skill | I practise speaking to improve my English. | tobulinti įgūdį |
| ask for clarification | Could you explain that again? | paprašyti paaiškinti |

After **want/need/plan/hope**, use **to + base verb**. After **enjoy/finish/avoid/practise**, use **verb-ing**. When you are unsure, record the whole chunk: **plan to apply**, **practise speaking**.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Mandagi komunikacija:</strong> kai užduotis neaiški, tinka <strong>Could you explain that again?</strong>, <strong>What does ... mean?</strong>, <strong>Do I need to ...?</strong> ir <strong>Could you show me an example?</strong>. Tai aiškiau ir mandagiau nei vienas žodis <strong>What?</strong></p>

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

Keep the final sound in **work** and **task** clear. In **working, learning, practising**, the ending is /ɪŋ/, not a separate strong syllable. Stress the key noun in workplace questions: **Which FILE do I open? When is the DEADLINE?**

## Exercise 4: Read a Course Email / Pratimas 4

Read first for the purpose of the email. Read again and underline **date, days, time, equipment, preparation, contact action**.

> Hello Rasa, Your evening computer course starts on 4 September. Classes run on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 18:00 to 19:30. Please bring a laptop. You need to install the free program before the first class. If you have trouble installing it, reply to this email. We look forward to meeting you.

1. When does the course start?
2. How often are the classes?
3. What must Rasa bring?
4. What should she do if installation is difficult?

## Exercise 5: Clarify a Task / Pratimas 5

a. Certainly. First, open the shared folder. b. Sorry, could you explain the first step again? c. Thanks. Do I need to rename the file? d. Yes, please use today's date.

## Exercise 6: Make a Learning Plan / Pratimas 6

Write six sentences with two goals, two activities you enjoy, one learning need, and one request for help. Model: **I want to improve my computer skills. I plan to take an evening course. I enjoy learning with other people. I enjoy practising at home. I need to understand spreadsheets. Could you show me how to make a chart?**

Add a specific schedule and a way to notice progress.

**Sentence starters:**
**I want to... because... / I plan to... on... / I enjoy... / I need to... / Could you show me...? / I will check my progress by...**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] I used two correct **to + verb** patterns.
- [ ] I used two correct **-ing** patterns.
- [ ] At least one goal includes a time or frequency.
- [ ] My request names the exact help I need.
- [ ] I included a realistic progress check.

### Workplace or classroom rehearsal / Repeticija

Imagine you hear: **Please update the shared file before the meeting.** Ask three clarification questions about **which file, what to change,** and **the deadline**. End by summarising the task in your own words.

## Exercise 8: The First Day at Work / Pratimas 8

Read the dialogue.

> **Manager:** Welcome, Eglė. This morning, I want you to read the safety guide.
> **Eglė:** Certainly. Where can I find it?
> **Manager:** It's in the shared folder under “Team Documents”. After that, please update the customer list.
> **Eglė:** Sorry, could you show me which file I need to update?
> **Manager:** Of course. It's this one. Please finish entering the new names before lunch.
> **Eglė:** Do I need to phone the customers too?
> **Manager:** Not today. You'll practise making calls with Tomas tomorrow.

1. What does Eglė need to read first?
2. Where can she find it?
3. What task comes next?
4. What does she ask the manager to show her?
5. When should she finish entering the names?
6. Does she need to call customers today?
7. What will she practise tomorrow?

## Exercise 10: Read, Cover, and Remember the Instructions / Pratimas 10

Ask a partner to read the instructions twice, or read them aloud yourself. Cover them before completing the notes.

> **Training instructions:** The customer-service workshop begins at 9:15 in Room 3. Bring a notebook, but you don't need to bring a laptop. First, you will practise greeting customers. After the break, you will work in pairs and answer sample phone calls. Please finish the online preparation task by Thursday evening.

1. Start time: _____
2. Room: _____
3. Item to bring: _____
4. Item not needed: _____
5. First activity: _____
6. Activity after the break: _____
7. Preparation deadline: _____

Compare your notes with the text. Which instruction would you ask the trainer to explain in more detail?

## Exercise 11: Clarify a Work or Study Task / Pratimas 11

Student A gives a three-step task; Student B asks at least three clarification questions. Then change roles. Choose a task such as uploading homework, preparing a room, sending an invoice, or joining an online class.

Useful questions: **Could you explain the first step again? Which file/room do I need? When is the deadline? Do I need to...? Could you show me an example?**

Model: **A: Please update the list and send it to me. B: Which list do I need to update? A: The June customer list. B: When is the deadline? A: Friday at twelve. B: Do I need to add the phone numbers? A: Yes, please.**

Success checklist:

- [ ] The task had a clear order and deadline.
- [ ] I asked at least three complete questions.
- [ ] I used one **to + verb** and one **verb-ing** chunk.
- [ ] I repeated the final instruction to check understanding.
