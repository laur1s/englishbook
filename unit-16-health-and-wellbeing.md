# Unit 16: Health and Wellbeing

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Describe common symptoms.
- Ask for and give simple health advice.
- Explain purpose with **to + verb**.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **I've got... / I have...** for a symptom: *I've got a sore throat.* Use **should/shouldn't** for advice. Imperatives give direct instructions: *Drink water. Don't drive.* Use **to + verb** for purpose: *Take this medicine to reduce the pain.* Seek professional help for serious or persistent symptoms.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Simptomui nusakyti tinka <strong>I've got... / I have...</strong>, patarimui – <strong>should/shouldn't</strong>, o tikslui – <strong>to + veiksmažodis</strong>. Jei sunku kvėpuoti, reikia skubios medicinos pagalbos; jei būklė sunki, pavyzdžiui, žmogus gaudo orą ar negali normaliai kalbėti, skambinkite <strong>112</strong>.</p>

## Exercise 2: Choose the Best Advice / Pratimas 2

1. You have a high temperature. You **should rest / should run**.
2. Your ankle hurts badly. **Walk on it / Don't put weight on it**.
3. Drink water **to stay / staying** hydrated.
4. You **shouldn't / should** take medicine that is not for you.
5. **Get / To get** urgent medical help if breathing is difficult.
6. I went to the pharmacy **to ask / ask** for advice.

## Exercise 3: Put the Instructions in Order / Pratimas 3

Put these steps for a pharmacy visit in one sensible order: **explain the symptom; say how long you have had it; listen to the advice; check how to use the medicine**.

## Exercise 4: Read a Pharmacy Dialogue / Pratimas 4

> A: I've had a cough for three days, and my throat hurts. B: Do you have a temperature? A: No. B: You can try these lozenges to calm your throat. Drink warm liquids and rest. Contact a doctor if the cough gets worse. Get urgent medical help if breathing is difficult. Call 112 if it is severe—for example, if you are gasping or cannot speak normally.

1. How long has the customer had a cough?
2. Do they have a temperature?
3. What are the lozenges for?
4. When should the customer contact a doctor, get urgent help, or call 112?

## Exercise 5: Complete the Advice / Pratimas 5

Use **should, shouldn't, to, don't**.

1. You _____ drink more water. 2. Take a break _____ rest your eyes. 3. _____ use the cream near your eyes. 4. You _____ ignore severe pain.

## Exercise 6: Create a Safe Advice Card / Pratimas 6

Choose a minor problem and give three safe suggestions plus one condition for professional help. Model: **For a mild headache, you should rest and drink water. Take a screen break to relax your eyes. You shouldn't drive if you feel dizzy. Contact a doctor if the pain is severe or continues. Call 112 for a life-threatening emergency.**
