# Unit 16: Health and Wellbeing

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Describe common symptoms.
- Ask for and give simple health advice.
- Explain purpose with **to + verb**.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Practise language for **minor everyday problems**, not diagnosis. Learn the symptom phrase, add how long it has continued, give cautious advice, and state when professional or emergency help is needed.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **I've got... / I have...** for a symptom: *I've got a sore throat.* Use **should/shouldn't** for advice. Imperatives give direct instructions: *Drink water. Don't drive.* Use **to + verb** for purpose: *Take this medicine to reduce the pain.* Seek professional help for serious or persistent symptoms.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Simptomui nusakyti tinka <strong>I've got... / I have...</strong>, patarimui – <strong>should/shouldn't</strong>, o tikslui – <strong>to + veiksmažodis</strong>. Jei sunku kvėpuoti, reikia skubios medicinos pagalbos; jei būklė sunki, pavyzdžiui, žmogus gaudo orą ar negali normaliai kalbėti, skambinkite <strong>112</strong>.</p>

### Build a clear symptom message / Aiškus simptomo apibūdinimas

| Information | Useful language |
| --- | --- |
| problem | **I've got a cough / My ankle hurts / I feel dizzy.** |
| duration | **for two days / since Monday / since this morning** |
| strength | **mild, quite bad, severe, getting worse** |
| relevant extra fact | **I don't have a temperature. / I fell yesterday.** |
| request | **What should I do? / Could I speak to a pharmacist?** |

**For** gives a period (*for two days*); **since** gives a starting point (*since Tuesday*).

### Advice is not a diagnosis / Patarimas nėra diagnozė

Use cautious language: **You should rest. You could ask a pharmacist. Contact a doctor if it continues.** Do not claim certainty about a medical condition. In a real emergency, follow local professional guidance.

### Model pharmacy exchange / Pokalbio vaistinėje pavyzdys

Say this exchange twice—first slowly, then naturally:

> A: I've had a sore throat since yesterday. What should I do?
> B: You should rest and drink warm liquids. Ask a pharmacist if you need advice about medicine.
> A: When should I contact a doctor?
> B: Contact one if it gets worse or continues.

### Symptom Questions and Useful Chunks / Klausimai apie simptomus

| Ask | Example answer | Lithuanian support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What's the problem? | I've got a sore throat. | Kuo skundžiatės? Man skauda gerklę. |
| How long have you had it? | For two days. | Kiek laiko tai tęsiasi? Dvi dienas. |
| Do you have a temperature? | Yes, 38°C. | Ar karščiuojate? |
| Are you allergic to anything? | Yes, I'm allergic to penicillin. | Ar kam nors alergiškas (-a)? |
| Are you taking any medicine? | Only painkillers. | Ar vartojate kokių nors vaistų? |

Useful collocations: **have a cough**, **have a headache**, **feel dizzy**, **feel sick**, **hurt your ankle**, **take medicine**, **follow the instructions**, **contact a doctor**. For medicine choice and dose, follow the label and advice from a pharmacist or doctor.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Kalbos ir saugumo pastaba:</strong> angliškai dažnai sakoma <strong>I have a headache</strong>, bet <strong>my ankle hurts</strong>. Mokomoji užduotis nepakeičia sveikatos specialisto patarimo. Vaistų dozę tikrinkite informaciniame lapelyje arba klauskite vaistininko ar gydytojo.</p>

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

Word stress helps a listener understand symptoms: **HEAD**ache, **STOM**ach ache, **TEM**perature, **MED**icine. In **I've got a sore throat**, join **got_a** smoothly. Say the key symptom a little more strongly: *I've got a **SORE THROAT**.*

## Exercise 2: Choose the Best Advice / Pratimas 2

1. You have a high temperature. You **should rest / should run**.
2. Your ankle hurts badly. **Walk on it / Don't put weight on it**.
3. Drink water **to stay / staying** hydrated.
4. You **shouldn't / should** take medicine that is not for you.
5. **Get / To get** urgent medical help if breathing is difficult.
6. I went to the pharmacy **to ask / ask** for advice.

## Exercise 3: Put the Instructions in Order / Pratimas 3

Put these steps for a pharmacy visit in one sensible order: **explain the symptom; say how long you have had it; listen to the advice; check how to use the medicine**.

## Exercise 4: Read a Pharmacy Dialogue / Pratimas 4

**Listening-style practice:** cover the dialogue. Ask someone or text-to-speech to read it once. Note only **duration, temperature, product, warning**. Then uncover it and check your four notes.

> A: I've had a cough for three days, and my throat hurts. B: Do you have a temperature? A: No. B: You can try these lozenges to calm your throat. Drink warm liquids and rest. Contact a doctor if the cough gets worse. Get urgent medical help if breathing is difficult. Call 112 if it is severe—for example, if you are gasping or cannot speak normally.

1. How long has the customer had a cough?
2. Do they have a temperature?
3. What are the lozenges for?
4. When should the customer contact a doctor, get urgent help, or call 112?

## Exercise 6: Create a Safe Advice Card / Pratimas 6

Choose a minor problem and give three safe suggestions plus one condition for professional help. Model: **For a mild headache, you should rest and drink water. Take a screen break to relax your eyes. You shouldn't drive if you feel dizzy. Contact a doctor if the pain is severe or continues. Call 112 for a life-threatening emergency.**

**Sentence starters:**
**For a mild... / You should... / You could... to... / You shouldn't... / Contact a professional if...**

**Safety and language checklist:**

- [ ] I chose a minor, familiar problem and did not diagnose it.
- [ ] I gave three cautious, practical suggestions.
- [ ] I used **to + verb** once to explain purpose.
- [ ] I included a clear condition for professional help.
- [ ] I separated routine advice from emergency action.

## Exercise 8: A Call to the Health Centre / Pratimas 8

Read the dialogue.

> **Receptionist:** Good morning. How can I help?
> **Mantas:** I'd like to speak to a nurse. I've had a bad headache since yesterday evening.
> **Receptionist:** Do you have any other symptoms?
> **Mantas:** I feel a little sick, but I don't have a temperature.
> **Receptionist:** Are you able to drink water?
> **Mantas:** Yes.
> **Receptionist:** A nurse can call you at 10:30. If the pain suddenly becomes severe or you become very unwell, get urgent medical help.
> **Mantas:** Thank you. I'll keep my phone with me.

1. Who does Mantas want to speak to?
2. When did the headache begin?
3. What other symptom does he report?
4. Does he have a temperature?
5. Can he drink water?
6. When can the nurse call?
7. What should he do if the pain suddenly becomes severe?

## Exercise 11: Pharmacy Role-Play / Pratimas 11

Student A has a minor symptom; Student B is a pharmacist. Use a real or imaginary problem. Do not diagnose. The pharmacist asks questions, gives cautious general advice, and explains when to contact a doctor.

**Customer starters:** *I've got... / I've had it for... / I am/I'm not allergic to...*
**Pharmacist starters:** *How long...? / Do you have...? / You should... / Follow the instructions... / Contact a doctor if...*

Model: **A: I've got a sore throat. I've had it for two days. B: Do you have a temperature? A: No. B: You should rest and drink warm liquids. You can ask a pharmacist about suitable products. Contact a doctor if it gets worse or continues.**
