# Unit 22: Services and Problem-Solving

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Explain a problem with a product or service.
- Use **too + adjective** and **adjective + enough**.
- Request a repair, replacement, or refund politely.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

A successful service conversation follows four steps: **greet → describe the purchase and problem → give evidence → request a solution**. Practise calm, specific language even when the problem is frustrating.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **too** before an adjective for more than acceptable: *The shoes are too small.* Use **enough** after an adjective: *They aren't wide enough.* Present Perfect can describe a current result: *The screen has stopped working.*

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> <strong>Too</strong> eina prieš būdvardį ir reiškia „per“: <strong>too small</strong> - <strong>per mažas</strong>, <strong>too noisy</strong> - <strong>per triukšmingas</strong>. <strong>Enough</strong> eina po būdvardžio ir reiškia „pakankamai“: <strong>wide enough</strong> - <strong>pakankamai platus</strong>. Pirmiausia apibūdinkite pirkinį ir problemą, tada pateikite įrodymą ir mandagiai paprašykite sprendimo.</p>

### When the first solution is unavailable / Kai pirmas sprendimas netinka

If the first solution is impossible, use:

- **Is there another option?**
- **Would it be possible to...?**
- **If you can't replace it, could I have a refund?**
- **So, you will contact me by Friday. Is that right?**

### A Calm Problem Report / Ramus pranešimas apie problemą

A useful complaint is not only polite; it is also specific. Include:

1. **purchase or service:** *I ordered a desk lamp from your website.*
2. **time:** *It arrived yesterday.*
3. **exact problem:** *The cable is missing.*
4. **evidence:** *My order number is 4816, and I have the receipt.*
5. **preferred solution:** *Could you send the missing cable, please?*
6. **acceptable alternative:** *If that is not possible, I'd like to return the lamp.*

| Problem | Possible solution | Lithuanian support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| faulty item | repair or replacement | taisymas arba pakeitimas |
| wrong size/colour | exchange | apkeitimas |
| unwanted suitable item | return, depending on the policy | grąžinimas pagal taisykles |
| money returned | refund | pinigų grąžinimas |
| late or lost parcel | delivery investigation | siuntos paieška / tyrimas |
| poor service | apology and correction | atsiprašymas ir problemos ištaisymas |

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Kalbos strategija:</strong> pradėkite <em>I'm afraid there's a problem with...</em> arba <em>Could you help me with...?</em>. Reikalavimas <em>Give me...</em> dažnai skamba grubiai. Vietoj jo vartokite <em>I'd like...</em> arba <em>Could you...?</em>.</p>

### Too, Enough, and Present Results / Too, enough ir dabartinis rezultatas

- **too + adjective:** *The room is too noisy.*
- **adjective + enough:** *The room isn't quiet enough.*
- **enough + noun:** *There aren't enough chairs.*
- **too much + uncountable noun:** *There is too much noise.*
- **too many + plural noun:** *There are too many delays.*

The Present Perfect can connect an earlier event to a problem now: *My order has not arrived. The handle has broken.* Give a finished past time with Past Simple: *I ordered it on Monday.* Compare: *The screen stopped working yesterday* and *The screen has stopped working* (the result matters now).

## Exercise 4: Read a Return Conversation / Pratimas 4

As you read, label the four moves: **problem, evidence, request, options**.

> Customer: I bought these headphones on Monday, but the left side has stopped working. Assistant: I'm sorry about that. Do you have the receipt? Customer: Yes. Could you replace them, please? Assistant: Certainly. We don't have the same colour, but you can choose the black ones or receive a refund.

1. When were the headphones bought?
2. What has happened?
3. What does the customer request first?
4. Which two solutions are available?

## Exercise 5: Improve the Complaint / Pratimas 5

Rewrite politely: **This is bad. Give me money. It doesn't work.** Include what you bought, the problem, evidence, and your preferred solution.

Model: **Hello. I bought this speaker here on Saturday, but it has stopped working. Here is my receipt. Could you replace it, please?**

**Checklist:** include a greeting, purchase detail, exact fault, evidence, and one polite request.

## Exercise 6: Role-Play a Service Problem / Pratimas 6

Model: **I bought this lamp last week, but it isn't bright enough and the switch has broken. Here is my receipt. Could you replace it, please? If that isn't possible, I'd like a refund.** Use a calm greeting, facts, and a clear request.

Choose one problem: **wrong hotel room, delayed delivery, damaged bag, or faulty headphones**. Speak for 1–2 minutes. If you study alone, record the customer first, pause, then record the assistant.

**Customer starters:** **Hello, I need help with... / I bought/booked... / The problem is... / Could you...? / If that isn't possible,...**
**Assistant starters:** **I'm sorry about that. / Could I see...? / We can... / Would that be suitable?**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] The customer gives a date, booking detail, receipt, or other evidence.
- [ ] The exact problem is clear.
- [ ] The request is polite and realistic.
- [ ] The assistant offers a solution and confirms the next step.
- [ ] The customer can respond if the first option is unsuitable.

Partner B should acknowledge the problem, ask for evidence, and offer one or two realistic solutions. Useful phrases: *I'm sorry about that. / May I see the receipt? / We can repair it by Friday. / Would a replacement be suitable?*

## Exercise 7: Read an Email and the Service Reply / Pratimas 7

> **Subject: Order 7042 – missing shelf part**
> Hello, I received a bookcase from your company on 6 June. The parcel was not damaged, but part C is missing, so the bookcase is not safe enough to use. I have checked all the packaging carefully. Could you send the missing part, please? I have attached a photo of the parts and my receipt. I need the bookcase before 15 June. Kind regards, Eglė
>
> **Reply**
> Dear Eglė, we're sorry that your order was incomplete. We have sent part C by express delivery, and it should arrive on 9 June. You do not need to return the other parts. Please contact us again if the parcel has not arrived by 10 June. Kind regards, HomeBox Support

1. When did Eglė receive the bookcase?
2. Why is it not safe enough to use?
3. Which two pieces of evidence has she provided?
4. Why does she mention 15 June?
5. What solution has the company chosen?
6. When should Eglė contact the company again?

## Exercise 9: Solve a Service Problem in Writing / Pratimas 9

Choose one situation: a cancelled class, an overcharged restaurant bill, a late parcel, or a faulty household item. Write a 90–120-word email.

**Planning frame:**

- *I am writing about...*
- *I booked/bought/ordered it on...*
- *Unfortunately,... has/have...*
- *This means that...*
- *Could you..., please?*
- *If that is not possible,...*

**Success checklist:** include a subject line, facts in time order, one **too/enough** phrase, evidence, a reasonable solution, and a polite closing. A different solution is valid if it fits the situation.
