# Unit 20: Invitations and Social Plans

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Invite, accept, decline, and suggest alternatives politely.
- Use **would like**, **Would you like...?**, **Let's**, and **How about...?**
- Confirm essential event details.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Move from a complete invitation to a useful response and then to negotiation. A social plan is complete only when everyone knows **what, when, where,** and the next action.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **Would you like to + verb?** for an invitation and **would like + noun/to + verb** for a preference. Use **Let's + base verb** or **How about + -ing?** for suggestions. A polite refusal often includes thanks and a short reason.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Kvieskite su <strong>Would you like to...?</strong>, siūlykite su <strong>Let's + bendratis</strong> arba <strong>How about + -ing?</strong>. Mandagus atsisakymas paprastai turi padėką, trumpą priežastį ir, jei įmanoma, kitą pasiūlymą.</p>

### A Complete Social Message / Išsami žinutė apie planus

A useful invitation answers the questions **what, when, where, how**, and sometimes **what to bring**:

> Would you like to join us for a walk on Sunday? We're meeting outside the library at 11. We can take bus 3 to the park. Bring a rain jacket because the weather may change.

Useful responses:

| Purpose | Phrase | Lithuanian support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| warm acceptance | I'd love to. / That sounds lovely. | Mielai. / Skamba puikiai. |
| check a detail | What time should I arrive? | Kuriuo metu turėčiau atvykti? |
| polite refusal | Thanks for inviting me, but I can't make it. | Ačiū už kvietimą, bet negalėsiu. |
| give a reason | I'm already working that evening. | Tą vakarą jau dirbu. |
| offer another plan | Could we meet on Sunday instead? | Gal galėtume susitikti sekmadienį? |
| confirm | Great, see you at the entrance at six. | Puiku, susitinkame prie įėjimo šeštą. |

**Can** is natural for an informal suggestion: *We can meet after work.* **Could** makes an option sound softer: *We could try the new café.* Do not answer a friendly invitation with only *yes* or *no* when more information would help.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Kultūrinė pastaba:</strong> trumpas atsisakymas nebūtinai yra nemandagus, tačiau anglų kalboje <strong>Thanks, but...</strong> ir alternatyva dažnai skamba šilčiau. Priežastis gali būti trumpa; asmeninių detalių pateikti neprivalote.</p>

### Arrangements and Suggestions / Susitarimai ir pasiūlymai

Use the Present Continuous for a fixed arrangement: *We're meeting at seven.* Use **going to** for an intention: *I'm going to bring a salad.* Use **maybe, perhaps, probably** when a plan is not certain: *I'll probably arrive at about six.*

Time phrases matter: **on Friday**, **at 6:30**, **from six to eight**, **by Thursday** (no later than Thursday), **until nine** (continuing to nine).

### Match the form to the social job / Forma pagal bendravimo tikslą

| Social job | Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| invite another person | **Would you like to + verb?** | Would you like to join us? |
| state your preference | **I'd like + noun / to + verb** | I'd like some tea. / I'd like to come. |
| suggest a shared action | **Let's + base verb** | Let's meet outside. |
| ask about an idea | **How about + -ing?** | How about meeting at six? |

**Common repairs:**

- *Would you like coming?* → **Would you like to come?**
- *How about to meet?* → **How about meeting?**
- *Let's meeting there* → **Let's meet there.**

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Formos atrama:</strong> po <strong>Would you like</strong> kvietime vartokite <strong>to + veiksmažodis</strong>, po <strong>How about</strong> – <strong>-ing</strong>, o po <strong>Let's</strong> – paprastąją veiksmažodžio formą. Mokykitės visą frazę kaip vieną modelį.</p>

### Mini-dialogue: refuse, repair, confirm

> A: Would you like to see the outdoor film on Friday?
>
> B: Thanks for asking, but I'm working until eight. How about Saturday?
>
> A: I can't make Saturday. We could have coffee on Sunday instead.
>
> B: That sounds good. What time shall we meet?
>
> A: Let's meet at three outside Green Table.
>
> B: Great—Sunday at three, outside Green Table. See you then.

The refusal is warm but brief. The speakers offer alternatives, check one practical detail, and repeat the final plan. **Instead** signals a replacement plan; **then** in *See you then* refers to the agreed time.

### Five-detail recall / Penkių detalių kartojimas

Use five fingers for **event – day – time – place – practical detail**. Read an invitation, cover it, and recall all five. Say contractions naturally: **I'd** /aɪd/, **we're**, **can't**. Let your voice rise slightly on the invitation and fall on the final confirmation: **Would you like to COME?** — **See you at THREE.**

## Exercise 1: Match Functions and Phrases / Pratimas 1

1. invite 2. accept 3. decline 4. suggest 5. confirm

a. That sounds great. b. So, we're meeting at seven, right? c. Would you like to come? d. Thanks, but I can't on Friday. e. How about meeting outside?

## Exercise 2: Choose the Correct Form / Pratimas 2

1. Would you like **to join / joining** us?
2. How about **to meet / meeting** at six?
3. Let's **take / taking** the bus.
4. I'd like **a / to** cup of tea.
5. I would like **visit / to visit** the exhibition.
6. **Do / Would** you like to come for dinner?

## Exercise 3: Rank the Responses / Pratimas 3

Give one reasonable ranking from most helpful to least helpful, then explain it. Different rankings are acceptable when the explanation discusses politeness and useful detail: **No. / Thanks, but I'm working. Could we meet on Sunday? / I can't. / I'd love to. What time?**

## Exercise 4: Read an Event Message / Pratimas 4

> Hi! Would you like to come to my board-game evening on Saturday? We are meeting at my flat at 6:30. Please bring a small snack if you can. The number 4 bus stops nearby. Let me know by Thursday because I need to prepare enough food.

1. What is the event?
2. When and where is it?
3. What can guests bring?
4. Why should guests reply by Thursday?

## Exercise 5: Complete Two Responses / Pratimas 5

Write one acceptance and one polite refusal. Include a question in the acceptance and an alternative in the refusal.

Models:

- **Acceptance:** *I'd love to come. What time should I arrive?*
- **Refusal:** *Thanks for inviting me, but I'm working on Saturday. Could we meet for coffee on Sunday instead?*

Check that each response sounds complete on its own and gives the organiser useful information.

## Exercise 6: Plan a Small Event / Pratimas 6

Write an invitation and a reply. Model: **Would you like to come to a picnic on Sunday at noon? We are meeting by the lake. Please bring a drink. — I'd love to. Should I bring a blanket too?**

Your invitation should include the event, day, time, meeting place, and one practical detail. Your reply should accept or decline warmly and include a useful follow-up question or alternative.

**Invitation starters:** **Would you like to...? / We're meeting... / Please bring... / Let me know by...**
**Reply starters:** **I'd love to... / Thanks, but... / Could we...? / So, I'll see you...**

## Exercise 7: Follow a Group Chat / Pratimas 7

> **Ieva:** Would you both like to come to the outdoor film on Friday? It starts at 9, but let's meet by the ticket office at 8:30.
> **Marco:** I'd love to. Should I buy my ticket online?
> **Ieva:** Yes, please. There may not be enough tickets at the door. Bring something warm too.
> **Amina:** Thanks for inviting me, but I'm working until 9 on Friday. How about the Saturday film instead?
> **Marco:** I can't do Saturday. We could all have coffee on Sunday afternoon.
> **Ieva:** Good idea. Is 3 p.m. at Green Table okay?
> **Amina:** Perfect. See you there!

1. Where and when should Ieva and Marco meet on Friday?
2. Why should Marco buy his ticket online?
3. What should he bring?
4. Why does Amina decline the Friday invitation?
5. Which first alternative does she suggest?
6. What final plan do all three people accept?

## Exercise 8: Complete the Social Functions / Pratimas 8

Choose from: **I'd love to · Would you like to · How about · Thanks, but · Let's · Could we**. Use each phrase once.

1. _____ join us for lunch tomorrow?
2. _____, but what time does it start?
3. _____ I can't come on Wednesday.
4. _____ meeting after work instead?
5. _____ book a table now.
6. _____ meet near the station rather than the café?

## Exercise 9: Negotiate a Plan / Pratimas 9

Work with these constraints: Ana is free on Saturday from 12:00 to 17:00; Mantas is free after 14:00; the museum closes at 17:00; rain is expected after 16:00; both want lunch and one activity. Write or perform an 8-line exchange that reaches a realistic agreement.

**Model pattern:** invitation → problem → alternative → preference → practical question → confirmation.

**Success checklist:**

- Use two different suggestion forms.
- Give a reason for one choice.
- Check time and place.
- End with a clear confirmation.
- Do not force one plan if another plan also respects all the constraints.

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can refuse politely and offer another time.
- [ ] I can distinguish a suggestion from a fixed arrangement.
- [ ] I can negotiate a plan when two people have different schedules.
