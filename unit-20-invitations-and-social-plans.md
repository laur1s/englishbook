# Unit 20: Invitations and Social Plans

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Invite, accept, decline, and suggest alternatives politely.
- Use **would like**, **Would you like...?**, **Let's**, and **How about...?**
- Confirm essential event details.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **Would you like to + verb?** for an invitation and **would like + noun/to + verb** for a preference. Use **Let's + base verb** or **How about + -ing?** for suggestions. A polite refusal often includes thanks and a short reason.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Kvieskite su <strong>Would you like to...?</strong>, siūlykite su <strong>Let's + bendratis</strong> arba <strong>How about + -ing?</strong>. Mandagus atsisakymas paprastai turi padėką, trumpą priežastį ir, jei įmanoma, kitą pasiūlymą.</p>

## Exercise 1: Match Functions and Phrases / Pratimas 1

1. invite 2. accept 3. decline 4. suggest 5. confirm

a. That sounds great. b. So, we're meeting at seven, right? c. Would you like to come? d. Thanks, but I can't on Friday. e. How about meeting outside?

## Exercise 2: Choose the Correct Form / Pratimas 2

1. Would you like **to join / joining** us?
2. How about **to meet / meeting** at six?
3. Let's **take / taking** the bus.
4. I'd like **a / to** cup of tea.
5. I would like **visit / to visit** the exhibition.
6. **Do / Would** you like to come for dinner?

## Exercise 3: Rank the Responses / Pratimas 3

Give one reasonable ranking from most helpful to least helpful, then explain it. Different rankings are acceptable when the explanation discusses politeness and useful detail: **No. / Thanks, but I'm working. Could we meet on Sunday? / I can't. / I'd love to. What time?**

## Exercise 4: Read an Event Message / Pratimas 4

> Hi! Would you like to come to my board-game evening on Saturday? We are meeting at my flat at 6:30. Please bring a small snack if you can. The number 4 bus stops nearby. Let me know by Thursday because I need to prepare enough food.

1. What is the event?
2. When and where is it?
3. What can guests bring?
4. Why should guests reply by Thursday?

## Exercise 5: Complete Two Responses / Pratimas 5

Write one acceptance and one polite refusal. Include a question in the acceptance and an alternative in the refusal.

## Exercise 6: Plan a Small Event / Pratimas 6

Write an invitation and a reply. Model: **Would you like to come to a picnic on Sunday at noon? We are meeting by the lake. Please bring a drink. — I'd love to. Should I bring a blanket too?**

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can invite someone and confirm details.
- [ ] I can refuse politely and offer another time.
