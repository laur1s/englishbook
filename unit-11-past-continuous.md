## Part 2: Grammar - Past Continuous Formation / Gramatika - Past Continuous formavimas

### Positive Form (Teigiamoji forma)
**Structure:** was/were + verb-ing

| Subject | Auxiliary | Verb-ing | Example |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| I | was | walking | I was walking home. |
| He/She/It | was | walking | She was walking home. |
| You/We/They | were | walking | They were walking home. |

**Examples:**
- I was eating dinner at 8 PM yesterday.
- She was studying when I called.
- They were playing football all afternoon.

### Negative Form (Neigiamoji forma)
**Structure:** was/were + not + verb-ing

- I was not (wasn't) eating.
- She was not (wasn't) studying.
- They were not (weren't) playing.

### Question Form (Klausimo forma)
**Structure:** Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?

- Was I eating?
- Was she studying?
- Were they playing?

**Short answers:**
- Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.
- Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
- Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.

## Part 3: Grammar - Past Simple vs Past Continuous / Gramatika - Past Simple vs Past Continuous

### Past Simple (Completed Actions)
- Used for completed actions in the past
- Focus on the action itself
- Often with time expressions: yesterday, last week, in 2010, when I was young

**Examples:**
- I ate dinner. (completed action)
- She studied English. (completed action)
- They played football. (completed action)

### Past Continuous (Ongoing Actions)
- Used for actions in progress at a specific time in the past
- Focus on the duration of the action
- Often with time expressions: at 5 PM, while, when, all day

**Examples:**
- I was eating dinner at 8 PM. (in progress at that time)
- She was studying when I called. (interrupted)
- They were playing football all afternoon. (ongoing)

### Key Differences (Pagrindiniai skirtumai)
1. **Interrupted actions:** Past Continuous + Past Simple
   - I was eating dinner when the phone rang.
   - She was studying when her friend called.

2. **Background vs main event:** Past Continuous for background
   - It was raining while we were playing football.
   - The sun was shining when I woke up.

3. **Specific time:** Past Continuous
   - At 5 PM yesterday, I was working.
   - Last night at 10 PM, she was sleeping.

## Additional Cultural Notes / Papildomos kultūrinės pastabos

- **Storytelling:** Past Continuous sets the scene, Past Simple advances the plot.
- **Emphasis:** It shows actions were in progress, not completed.
- **Lithuanian Difference:** Lithuanian often uses present for vivid past narration.

---

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

- English speakers use Past Continuous to set the scene in stories
- "What were you doing when...?" is a common way to ask about specific moments
- In Lithuanian, present tense can describe past events; English needs specific past tenses
- Past Continuous emphasizes the duration and interruption of actions
