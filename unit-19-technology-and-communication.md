# Unit 19: Technology and Communication

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Explain a simple technical process.
- Form common adverbs of manner.
- Write a clear message and follow basic online-safety advice.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Name a precise problem, put safe actions in order, and confirm the result. Complete the unit by giving instructions that another learner can actually follow without guessing.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Many adverbs use adjective + **-ly**: *careful → carefully*. Common irregular forms include *good → well* and *fast → fast*. Use **first, next, then, finally** to order instructions.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Būdo prieveiksmis atsako į klausimą „kaip?“: <strong>carefully, clearly</strong>. Išimtys: <strong>good → well</strong>, <strong>fast → fast</strong>. Instrukcijas tvarkykite žodžiais <strong>first, next, then, finally</strong>.</p>

### Everyday Technology Language / Kasdienė technologijų kalba

| Action | Useful phrase | Lithuanian support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| start again | restart the device | paleisti įrenginį iš naujo |
| enter an account | log in / sign in | prisijungti |
| leave an account | log out / sign out | atsijungti |
| make a second copy | back up a file | sukurti atsarginę failo kopiją |
| put a newer version on a device | update an app | atnaujinti programėlę |
| make a device work with Wi-Fi | connect to a network | prisijungti prie tinklo |
| remove a file | delete a file | ištrinti failą |
| protect an account | turn on two-step verification | įjungti patvirtinimą dviem veiksmais |

Technology conversations usually have four parts:

1. **Name the goal:** *I'm trying to upload a document.*
2. **Describe the result:** *The page stops at 80%.*
3. **Say what you have tried:** *I've restarted the app.*
4. **Ask for one clear action:** *What should I try next?*

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Mokymosi strategija:</strong> nesakykite tik <em>It doesn't work</em>. Pasakykite, ką norite padaryti, ką matote ekrane ir ką jau bandėte. Taip pašnekovui lengviau padėti.</p>

### Clear and Safe Instructions / Aiškios ir saugios instrukcijos

Use an imperative for a direct step: *Open Settings. Choose Privacy.* Add **please** when you ask another person to do something: *Please send the link again.* Use **don't** for a warning: *Don't open the attachment if you do not know the sender.*

Notice the difference:

- *The message is clear.* (**clear** describes a noun.)
- *She explains it clearly.* (**clearly** describes an action.)
- *He is a careful user.* / *He checks links carefully.*
- *The connection is bad.* / *The camera works badly.*

Some everyday adverbs do not end in **-ly**: *fast, hard, late, early*. **Hardly** has a different meaning: *I can hardly hear you* means *I can almost not hear you*.

### Similar actions, different meanings / Panašūs, bet skirtingi veiksmai

| Action | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **sign up** | create a new account | I signed up with my email address. |
| **sign in / log in** | enter an existing account | Sign in, but never share your password. |
| **upload** | send a file from your device to a service | Upload the form to the course page. |
| **download** | copy a file from a service to your device | Download the timetable and save it. |
| **attach** | add a file to a message | Attach the photo before you send the email. |
| **share a link** | give access through a web address | Check who can open the link before sharing it. |

**Common repairs:**

- *I entered to my account* → **I signed in to my account.**
- *I downloaded the photo to the website* → **I uploaded the photo to the website.**

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Reikšmės atrama:</strong> <strong>upload</strong> reiškia siųsti failą iš savo įrenginio į sistemą, o <strong>download</strong> – atsisiųsti į savo įrenginį. <strong>Sign up</strong> kuriant paskyrą skiriasi nuo <strong>sign in</strong> jungiantis prie jau turimos paskyros.</p>

### Mini-dialogue: diagnose, act, confirm

> A: I'm trying to upload my homework, but the button is grey.
>
> B: Have you chosen a file?
>
> A: Yes, but it is very large.
>
> B: Check the size carefully. Then save a smaller copy and try again. Don't include private information in the file name.
>
> A: It has uploaded now. Can you see it?
>
> B: Yes, it opens correctly.

Notice the sequence: **goal → visible problem → checking question → safe action → confirmation**. If a step fails, ask **What can you see now?** before choosing the next action.

### Say, cover, recall / Pasakykite, uždenkite, prisiminkite

Stress the action words: **OPEN Settings. CHECK the recipient. ATTACH the file. PRESS Send.** Read four instructions aloud, cover them, and repeat them with **first, next, then, finally**, plus one **Don't...** warning.

## Exercise 3: Sequence the Instructions / Pratimas 3

Put these steps in one safe, logical order: **choose the file; open a new message; check the recipient; attach the file; press send**. Add **first, next, then, finally**. The recipient must be checked before you press send.

## Exercise 4: Read a Support Chat / Pratimas 4

Read once to identify the two problems. Read again and number every action in the order Support recommends it.

> User: I can't join the video call. The screen is black. Support: First, check that the camera is on. Next, close other apps that use the camera. Then leave the call and join again. User: It works now, but people can't hear me. Support: Open settings and choose the correct microphone.

1. What was the first problem?
2. What should the user close?
3. Did joining again solve the camera problem?
4. Which setting should the user check next?

## Exercise 5: Spot the Unsafe Action / Pratimas 5

Choose and correct the unsafe action: **use a different password for important accounts; check links carefully; send your password to a stranger; update apps regularly**.

## Exercise 6: Write Helpful Instructions / Pratimas 6

Explain how to send a photo or join a call in 5–7 steps. Model: **First, open the app. Next, choose the person carefully. Then press the camera icon and select a photo. Check the photo before you send it. Finally, press send.**

Use this checklist:

- Start with the goal: *To join the call...*
- Use at least three sequence words.
- Give one safety check or useful warning.
- Use an imperative and one adverb.
- Ask another learner to follow the steps. Change any step that is unclear.

**Sentence starters:** **To..., first... / Next, ... carefully. / Before you..., check... / Don't... / Finally,...**

If you study alone, wait five minutes and follow only your written steps. Any action you have to remember but did not write is a missing step.

## Exercise 7: Read a Workplace Support Exchange / Pratimas 7

> **Rūta:** Hi Ben. I'm trying to send the monthly report, but the file is too large for email. I've already restarted my laptop, but that did not help.
> **Ben:** No problem. First, save the report in the team folder. Then click **Share** and copy the link. Check that only people in our team can open it.
> **Rūta:** Should I put the link in a new email?
> **Ben:** Yes. Please write the month in the subject line and send it to Lina and me. Don't post the link in the public chat.
> **Rūta:** Thanks. I've shared it successfully. Can you check that you can open it?
> **Ben:** It opens perfectly. Well done!

1. Why can Rūta not attach the report to an email?
2. Where should she save the report?
3. Which access setting should she check?
4. Who should receive the link?
5. Where must she not post it?
6. Which sentence confirms that the task is complete?

## Exercise 8: Choose the Best Support Reply / Pratimas 8

Choose the most useful reply.

1. *I can't log in.* — a. That's bad. b. Check your email address carefully, then reset the password if necessary. c. Click everything.
2. *The sound is very quiet.* — a. First, check the volume and the selected speaker. b. Buy a computer. c. Send me your password.
3. *A stranger has sent me a prize link.* — a. Open it quickly. b. Forward it to everyone. c. Don't open it; report and delete the message.
4. *My document has disappeared.* — a. Look in **Recent files** and the bin first. b. It is impossible. c. Type more slowly.
5. *The video freezes during calls.* — a. Speak loudly. b. Close unused apps and check the internet connection. c. Share your account.
6. *I need to leave a shared computer.* — a. Log out of your account. b. Leave every page open. c. Turn off two-step verification.

## Exercise 9: Give and Check Technical Help / Pratimas 9

Choose one situation: joining an online appointment, sharing a document, changing notification settings, or reporting a suspicious message. Prepare a 6–8-line support dialogue.

**Sentence starters:** *What are you trying to do? / What happens when you...? / Have you tried...? / First, ... / Be careful not to... / It works now, thank you.*

**Model:**

> A: I'm trying to join my doctor's video appointment, but I can't hear anything.
> B: First, check the sound icon carefully. Is it muted?
> A: No, it isn't.
> B: Then open the audio settings and choose your headphones.
> A: Great, it works now. Thank you.

Self-check: Is the problem specific? Are the steps in a safe order? Does the final line confirm the result?

Add one repair question if the first instruction does not work: **What can you see now? / Did an error message appear? / Shall we try another option?**
