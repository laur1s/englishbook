### Story

*Pasakojimas*

Seattle was hit by its worst storm in many years. Very strong winds moved across the city, and heavy rain hit the windows. Trees fell, roads flooded, and some power lines came down. People were told to stay inside if they could.

Dr. Meredith Grey was working the night shift at Seattle Grace Hospital. At first, the lights stayed on. Then the main power went out. The hospital became dark for a few seconds before the backup generators started. Small emergency lights shone in the halls, but some rooms were still quite dark.

"The generators can power the most important machines," a hospital engineer explained. "They can't power everything."

Meredith took a deep breath. The team needed to stay calm.

Soon, ambulances began to arrive. A construction worker fell from a work platform and badly hurt his arm. A mother and her teenage daughter were dangerously cold after their car left the road. Their clothes were wet, and their bodies were very cold. An older man had chest pain and signs of a heart attack.

More patients were waiting outside. The hospital could not help every person at the same time, so the team had to choose who needed help first. They treated the people in greatest danger first.

Meredith stood at the emergency desk. "The worker goes to Trauma Room One," she said. "The mother and daughter go to Room Two. Please start warming them slowly. Alex, can you check the man with chest pain?"

Alex nodded. "I can take him now."

A nurse looked worried. "What about the people with smaller cuts?"

"They have to wait, but someone must check them regularly," Meredith replied. "Tell them why there is a delay. If their condition changes, tell me immediately."

Meredith did not like making people wait. However, she knew the team had to use its limited rooms and equipment carefully.

Cristina examined the construction worker's arm. A major blood vessel in his arm was cut, so he needed surgery at once.

"Can you save my arm?" he asked.

Cristina spoke clearly. "We are going to try. The operation has risks, but waiting is more dangerous. I will explain the plan, and you can ask me questions."

After he agreed to the operation, the team prepared to move him. The main lamps were not working, so Cristina stopped the move. A repair team connected another operating room to a stable generator line. Nurses tested the lights and every important machine before Cristina began.

"We must not operate until the safety check is complete," Cristina said. A nurse stayed with the patient and kept checking his arm.

The operation was long, but it went as planned. Cristina treated the injury. The worker needed more checks and a recovery plan, and the team could not promise that his arm would be exactly the same.

While Cristina was operating, Izzie looked after frightened patients. A young boy named Ben was crying near the front desk. He had become separated from his parents when an ambulance brought him to the hospital.

Izzie sat beside him. "You are safe inside the hospital," she said. "We are looking for your parents. Can you tell me their names?"

Ben gave her the names. "Will they find me?"

"We are going to keep trying," Izzie replied. She did not promise something she could not control. "You can stay here with me while we call."

Ben held her hand. Izzie gave his information to the reception team, and they contacted other emergency centres.

In another room, Alex treated an older woman named Mrs. Lee. Flood water entered her basement, and she was trapped there until a neighbour helped her out.

"I thought I was going to die," she whispered.

Alex pulled a chair closer. "That sounds very frightening," he said. "You are safe now. We are going to check your breathing and keep you warm. Is there anyone you want us to call?"

Mrs. Lee gave him her daughter's number. When Alex called, the daughter began to cry with relief.

The storm continued for eighteen hours. The doctors and nurses worked in short teams so that each person could drink water, eat something, and rest for a few minutes. Meredith checked the medicine, blankets, and clean water. When one area began to run out of blankets, she moved supplies from a quieter floor.

At three in the morning, water started to come through part of the emergency-room ceiling. Meredith and two nurses moved the waiting patients to the cafeteria. They set up chairs, screens, and a small treatment table.

"This is not a normal waiting room," Meredith told the patients, "but we can carry on safely here."

Near dawn, Izzie received good news. Ben's parents were at another hospital. The roads were still closed, but the family could speak by phone.

"We are safe," Ben told them. "Dr. Izzie is staying with me."

When the sky became lighter, the wind finally became quieter. The hospital had cracked windows and several leaks, but the building was safe. Many patients were stable, but some still needed urgent treatment. The team continued to give families careful updates.

Meredith found her friends in the cafeteria. They were exhausted. Cristina was drinking water, Alex was calling Mrs. Lee's daughter again, and Izzie was helping Ben draw a picture for his parents.

"We kept going," Meredith said.

Cristina nodded. "Because everyone did one clear job at a time."

Alex gave a small smile. "I also made three cups of coffee. That was important work."

Izzie laughed. "The nurses were the real heroes. They kept the whole hospital moving."

Meredith agreed. No one person saved the night. The storm showed them what they could do together. They could not stop the wind or fix every problem, but they could listen, explain, make careful choices, and look after one another.

As repair teams began their work, Meredith looked around the busy hospital. The storm damaged the building, but it brought the team closer. Courage, she learned, could mean stopping for a safety check instead of rushing ahead.

### Fun Activities

*Smagios užduotys*

#### Activity 1: Safe-Action Sort / Saugių veiksmų rūšiavimas

Put each action under **must**, **can**, **don't have to**, or **mustn't**: **check essential machines; explain a delay; promise an unknown result; operate before the lights are safe; ask for help; use every machine at once**.

*Model:* "Staff must check essential machines. They mustn't promise an unknown result."

#### Activity 2: Storm Role-Play / Vaidmenų pokalbis per audrą

Practice with a partner:

Student A is a frightened patient during the storm. Student B is a doctor or nurse who must stay calm and explain what is happening.

Write your own short dialog:

"________________________________________________________________"
"________________________________________________________________"

*Model:* "The power is out, but the emergency generator is working. We have to check the most urgent patients first."
