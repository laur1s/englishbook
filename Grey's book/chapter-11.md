### Story

*Pasakojimas*

Six weeks later, Izzie stood outside a small clinic in the mountains. She accepted Elena's offer and joined the mission. The building had two rooms, a metal roof, and one satellite phone. There was no laboratory and no large hospital nearby.

On her first morning, Izzie was checking boxes of medicine while Elena was showing a nurse named Ana where to keep the new supplies. A worried father ran through the door with his ten-year-old son, Mateo. The boy's face was red, and his lips were swollen.

"He ate something at a village celebration," Ana explained. "Now he is having trouble breathing."

Izzie examined him quickly. "This may be a serious allergy. We have to act now."

Izzie called Elena at once. Together with Ana, they followed the clinic's emergency plan. Trained staff gave urgent care and arranged transport to the regional hospital. Izzie picked up the satellite phone to give the hospital a clear handover and confirm where the transport team would meet them.

The signal came and went. A doctor answered, but his voice was difficult to hear.

"This is Dr. Stevens at San Lucas clinic," Izzie said. "I am calling about an urgent transfer for a child with a possible serious allergic reaction."

"Please give me his name, age, symptoms, known allergies, and the care already given," the doctor replied.

Izzie gave the information, but the line broke. She called back and started again. This time, she wrote down the receiving doctor's name, the meeting place, the expected transport time, and the signs the team had to report immediately.

"Could you repeat the meeting place slowly, please?" she asked. "Did you say the east entrance?"

"Yes. Please confirm the child's name and the urgent symptoms."

Izzie repeated Mateo's name and symptoms. "Can you spell the receiving doctor's surname?"

The doctor spelled it. Izzie repeated the full message: the meeting place, the doctor's name, the expected time, and what changes to report. Only then did she end the call.

Mateo's father spoke very little English. He watched Izzie and Ana with fear in his eyes. Ana could interpret in his language, but first she needed a clear, simple message from Izzie. Ana introduced herself and asked if he wanted her help. He said yes.

"Tell him this," Izzie said. "We have started emergency care. Mateo has to go to the regional hospital for continued monitoring. Transport is coming now. His father can travel with him and doesn't have to pay the clinic today."

Ana passed on the message. The father asked several questions. Ana listened, then explained that he was worried about transport home and food for his other children.

Izzie faced Mateo's father while she spoke, not Ana. She paused after each short sentence so Ana could interpret. Then she asked Ana to pass on a question: "Can you tell me what you understand about Mateo's treatment?"

His father explained the plan in his own words. He understood that the team started urgent care, but he did not understand why Mateo still needed the regional hospital if he began to feel better.

"The hospital team needs to continue monitoring him after a serious reaction," Izzie explained. "The transport team can also help if his condition changes on the way."

Izzie looked at him and spoke slowly. "You don't have to leave Mateo. We can call your family. We have some food here."

When Ana translated, the father's shoulders dropped. He began to calm down.

After the father agreed to the plan, the team continued the clinic's emergency procedure and watched Mateo closely. The room was very quiet. His breathing became easier, but Izzie reminded everyone that the transfer was still necessary.

"That change is encouraging," Elena said. She checked Izzie's written handover. "You confirmed every important detail, and the transport team is on its way."

"The call was harder than I expected," Izzie admitted. "I could hear only half the words."

"You did not pretend to understand," Elena replied. "You asked the doctor to repeat, you wrote the message down, and you checked it. That is good medicine."

Elena also thanked Ana. "You made sure Mateo's father could ask questions and make a decision," she said.

Ana nodded. "Interpreting is more than changing words from one language to another. I also have to keep the message complete. I cannot add my own opinion or leave out a difficult part."

Izzie wrote that idea in her notebook. In a hospital with many machines, communication was important. In a two-room clinic, it could be one of the most important tools they had.

While they waited for transport, Mateo sat up and drew a picture. His father gave it to Izzie. It showed the clinic under a large yellow sun, with a telephone line crossing three mountains.

"For you," Ana translated.

Izzie smiled. Soon, the transport team arrived and Ana gave them the written handover. At Seattle Grace, Izzie had often thought that a good doctor needed every answer. Here she was learning something different. Sometimes a good doctor needed to ask a clear question, listen carefully, and connect a patient with the next safe level of care.

### Creative Questions

*Kūrybiniai klausimai*

1. Why is it useful to repeat important information?
   - *Model:* "It is useful because both people can find a mistake."
   - *Start:* "It is useful because _______________."
2. When do you ask someone to speak more slowly?
   - *Model:* "I ask when the signal is poor or the message is urgent."
   - *Start:* "I ask someone to speak more slowly when _______________."
3. How can you make instructions easier to understand?
   - *Model:* "I can use short sentences and pause between ideas."
   - *Start:* "I can use short sentences and _______________."
4. Would you like to work in a small clinic? Why?

   - *Start:* "I would / wouldn't like it because _______________."
5. Write one calm sentence for Mateo's father.
   - *Model:* "We have started urgent care, and transport is coming."
   - *Start:* "We have _______________, and _______________."

### Fun Activities

*Smagios užduotys*

#### Activity 1: Telephone Handover / Informacijos perdavimas telefonu

Student A reads: **Mateo, age 10, swollen lips, difficult breathing, urgent care started, transfer requested.** Student B asks for repetition, writes the details, and repeats them back. Use: **Could you repeat that? Could you spell it? Let me confirm...**

#### Activity 2: Pass On the Message / Perduokite žinutę

Explain this message in three shorter sentences: **Mateo must go to the regional hospital for continued monitoring, but his father does not need to leave and can travel with him.**

Model: **Mateo has to go to the regional hospital. His father can travel with him. His father doesn't have to leave.**
