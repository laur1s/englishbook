## Chapter 11: The Call Across the Mountains

## 11 Skyrius: Skambutis per kalnus

### Story

Six weeks later, Izzie stood outside a small clinic in the mountains. She had accepted Elena's offer. The building had two rooms, a metal roof, and one satellite phone. There was no laboratory and no large hospital nearby.

On her first morning, Izzie was checking boxes of medicine while Elena was showing a nurse named Ana where to keep the new supplies. A worried father ran through the door with his ten-year-old son, Mateo. The boy's face was red, and his lips were swollen.

"He ate something at a village celebration," Ana explained. "Now he is having trouble breathing."

Izzie examined him quickly. "This may be a serious allergy. We have to act now."

The clinic had the medicine they needed, but the label showed two possible doses. Mateo was small for his age, and Izzie wanted to confirm the correct amount with the regional hospital. Elena was helping another patient, so Izzie picked up the satellite phone herself.

The signal came and went. A doctor answered, but his voice was difficult to hear.

"This is Dr. Stevens at San Lucas clinic," Izzie said. "I need advice about a child with a possible allergic reaction."

"Please give me his age and weight," the doctor replied.

Izzie gave the information, but the line broke. She called back and started again. This time, she wrote down every instruction.

"Could you repeat the dose slowly, please?" she asked. "Did you say zero point one five?"

"Yes. Zero point one five milligrams for each kilogram. Please confirm his weight."

"Twenty kilograms," Izzie said. "Can you spell the name of the second medicine?"

The doctor spelled it. Izzie repeated the full message: the dose, the second medicine, and the signs she had to watch. Only then did she end the call.

Mateo's father spoke very little English. He watched Izzie and Ana with fear in his eyes. Ana could explain the instructions in his language, but first she needed a clear, simple message from Izzie.

"Tell him this," Izzie said. "Mateo needs the medicine now. He has to stay here for four hours. His father doesn't have to pay today. If Mateo's breathing gets worse, we will move him to the regional hospital."

Ana passed on the message. The father asked several questions. Ana listened, then explained that he was worried about transport home and food for his other children.

Izzie looked at him and spoke slowly. "You don't have to leave Mateo. We can call your family. We have some food here."

When Ana translated, the father's shoulders dropped. He began to calm down.

Izzie gave Mateo the medicine. For several minutes, everyone watched his breathing. The room was very quiet. Then the swelling slowly became smaller. Mateo opened his eyes and asked for water.

"That is a good sign," Elena said as she entered. She checked Izzie's written notes and the medicine label. "You confirmed every important detail."

"The call was harder than I expected," Izzie admitted. "I could hear only half the words."

"You did not pretend to understand," Elena replied. "You asked the doctor to repeat, you wrote the message down, and you checked it. That is good medicine."

Later, Mateo was sitting up and drawing a picture. His father gave the picture to Izzie. It showed the clinic under a large yellow sun, with a telephone line crossing three mountains.

"For you," Ana translated.

Izzie smiled. At Seattle Grace, she had often thought that a good doctor needed every answer. Here she was learning something different. Sometimes a good doctor needed to ask a clear question, listen carefully, and help one person understand another.

### Creative Questions

1. Why is it useful to repeat important information? Model: **It is useful because both people can find a mistake.**
2. When do you ask someone to speak more slowly? Start: **I ask someone to speak more slowly when...**
3. How can you make instructions easier to understand? Start: **I can use short sentences and...**
4. Would you like to work in a small clinic? Why?
5. Write one calm sentence for Mateo's father.

### Fun Activities

#### Activity 1: Telephone Handover

Student A reads: **Mateo, age 10, 20 kg, swollen lips, difficult breathing.** Student B asks for repetition, writes the details, and repeats them back. Use: **Could you repeat that? Could you spell it? Let me confirm...**

#### Activity 2: Pass On the Message

Explain this message in three shorter sentences: **Mateo must remain at the clinic while the team watches his breathing, but his father does not need to pay today and can stay with him.**

Model: **Mateo has to stay at the clinic. His father can stay too. He doesn't have to pay today.**

### Grammar Practice

1. Complete with **was/were + -ing** or Past Simple.

2. Ana _____ (translate) while Izzie _____ (watch) Mateo.
