### Story

*Pasakojimas*

Dr. Meredith Grey woke up before her alarm. It was still dark outside, but she could not sleep again. Today was her first day at Seattle Grace Hospital. Her heart was beating fast. She was excited, but she was also very nervous.

She made coffee and tried to eat some toast. She was not hungry. Then she put on clean clothes and packed her white coat in a bag. Before she left, she looked in the mirror.

"You studied for this," she told herself. "You can do it."

The hospital was huge and very busy. People in blue and green scrubs were walking quickly through the halls. Nurses were pushing beds. Doctors were talking on phones. A family was asking for directions near the front desk. Everything smelled like medicine and cleaning products.

Meredith checked in and found the locker room. Five new interns were starting that morning. One person was reading a hospital map. Another person was checking a list again and again. Everyone looked a little scared.

A smiling woman came to Meredith. "Hi, I'm Izzie Stevens," she said. "Is this your first day too?"

"Yes. I'm Meredith. I think I'm in the right place."

"Good," Izzie said. "Then I am too."

Before they could talk more, a tall man with gray hair entered the room. It was Dr. Webber, the chief of surgery. The interns became quiet.

"Welcome to Seattle Grace," Dr. Webber said in a serious voice. "You will work very hard here. You will learn something every day. You will also make mistakes. When that happens, tell the truth and ask for help. Your patients must come first."

Meredith listened carefully. She wanted to remember every word.

After the welcome talk, a nurse named Bailey gave the interns their jobs. "Ask if you do not understand," she told them. "Do not guess. We work as a team here."

Meredith's first job was to check a patient. Mr. Johnson was an older man with white hair. He had hip surgery the day before. Meredith stopped outside his room and read his name twice. Then she washed her hands and knocked on the open door.

"Hello, Mr. Johnson. I'm Dr. Grey," she said. She tried to sound confident. "May I check how you are doing?"

Mr. Johnson looked at her. "You look very young. Are you really a doctor?"

For a moment, Meredith did not know what to say. Then she smiled. "Yes, I am. I am a new doctor, so I also work with an experienced team."

Mr. Johnson laughed. "All right, Dr. Grey. The nurses here are very tough, so listen to them."

"That sounds like good advice," Meredith said.

She asked about his pain and checked his temperature, blood pressure, and pulse. His temperature and pulse were normal. His blood pressure was a little lower than the morning result.

Meredith looked at the numbers again. She did not want to hide her question. "I am going to ask Nurse Bailey to check this with me," she explained. "I want to be careful."

Mr. Johnson nodded. "Thank you for telling me."

Bailey came into the room and checked the result. She asked Mr. Johnson to drink some water, then showed Meredith how to record the information on the computer.

"You did the right thing," Bailey said outside the room. "A good doctor does not need to know everything. A good doctor knows when to ask."

Meredith felt calmer after that.

At noon, she went to the cafeteria. She almost sat alone, but a woman with dark hair called her over.

"Hi, I'm Cristina Yang," the woman said. "You're new, right?"

"Yes, I'm Meredith Grey. First day," she answered.

Izzie was sitting at the same table. She moved her bag so Meredith could sit down. Cristina pointed to another intern nearby. "That is Alex Karev. He makes a lot of jokes. Some of them are not very friendly."

Alex heard her. "I am friendly when I have coffee," he said.

Izzie smiled. "Welcome to the team, Meredith. What area do you want to study?"

"Surgery," Meredith said. "I want to learn everything, but this morning I learned that I also need to ask questions."

"That is useful," Izzie said. "I have already asked about ten."

Cristina opened a medical book beside her lunch. "I want to learn the answers before anyone asks me."

Alex laughed. "Good luck to all of us. This hospital is difficult."

After lunch, Meredith checked more patients. She wrote reports and learned how to use the hospital computer. She carried a message to a nurse and helped an older patient find the correct room. Once, she walked into the wrong lift and arrived on another floor. She felt embarrassed, but a cleaner showed her the way back.

Near the end of the day, Meredith visited Mr. Johnson again. His blood pressure was better, and he was sitting in a chair.

"You came back," he said.

"Of course," Meredith replied. "How is your pain now?"

"Better. You look less nervous too."

Meredith smiled. "A little less."

The day was very long. By evening, her feet hurt and her coffee was cold. She was exhausted, but she was also proud. She did not do anything famous. She listened, checked, asked for help, and looked after her patients. For a first day, that was enough.

When she drove home, Meredith thought about Dr. Webber's words and Bailey's advice. The hospital was difficult and big, but she felt happy there.

"This is only the beginning," she thought. "Tomorrow I will know one thing more."

She did not yet know how much the hospital, the patients, and her new team would change her life.
