## Chapter 12: A Plan That Changed

## 12 Skyrius: Pasikeitęs planas

### Story

On Friday evening, the clinic team was preparing for a vaccination day in a village across the valley. Elena created a group chat because the team would travel in two vehicles. Izzie posted the plan in short messages.

**Izzie:** We are meeting at the clinic at 6:30 tomorrow. The first jeep is leaving at 7:00. Please check in when you arrive.

**Ana:** I am bringing the appointment list and water.

**Elena:** I have the medicine. Who is collecting the cool boxes?

**Izzie:** I am. I will also charge the satellite phone tonight.

The plan looked simple. Forty children had appointments, and the village had prepared its community hall. If the team left at seven, they would arrive before nine.

At 5:45 on Saturday morning, heavy rain hit the clinic roof. Izzie's phone began to show new messages.

**Driver Luis:** Important update. A landslide has blocked the north road. We can't use our normal route.

**Ana:** Is everyone safe?

**Luis:** Yes. I am checking the south road now.

Izzie read the messages twice. Cancelling would be easy, but many families had walked a long way for the vaccination day. She called Elena.

"We need a new plan," Izzie said. "If the south road is open, we can still go, but we will be late."

"Ask the village team what they need," Elena replied. "Don't decide for them."

Izzie sent a message to Rosa, the village organiser. She explained the road problem and gave two options: start later or move the event to Sunday.

Rosa replied: **Families are already arriving. A later start is better. If you arrive by eleven, we will carry on today. Please send an update when you leave.**

The south road was open, but only one jeep was available. The team could not take every box and every person. In the clinic office, they compared the supply list.

"The large table is useful, but it is not as important as the medicine," Ana said.

"We can borrow tables there," Luis added in the chat.

Izzie removed the table and two heavy bags. Then she noticed another problem: one cool-box battery had not charged.

"We have three cool boxes, but only two are cold enough," she said.

Elena checked the appointment list. "Two boxes are enough for today's patients. Leave the third one here."

At 8:10, Izzie posted the new arrangement: **We are leaving at 8:30 in one jeep. We are bringing two cool boxes. We expect to arrive at 10:45.**

Rosa answered with a thumbs-up and a short message: **Thank you. I will tell the families. Drive carefully.**

The journey was slow. Luis drove carefully around water and stones. Every thirty minutes, Ana checked the temperature inside the cool boxes. Izzie sent brief updates when the signal was available.

They reached the village at 10:50. Families were waiting in the community hall, but no one was angry. Rosa had passed on the team's messages and changed the appointment order. Children who lived farthest away went first.

The team set up two small tables. Izzie explained each step to the families, and Ana answered questions. By late afternoon, all forty children had received care.

On the drive home, Elena looked at the long group-chat history. "You gave clear updates, offered choices, and confirmed the new arrangement," she said.

"I wanted to solve everything myself," Izzie admitted.

"But you didn't," Elena said. "You worked with the people who were affected. That is why the plan worked out."

Izzie watched the mountains through the wet window. The day had not followed the first plan, but it had still succeeded. Good teamwork was not about controlling every problem. It was about sharing useful information, listening to replies, and changing the plan together.

### Understanding Questions

1. What event was the team preparing for?
2. Why did the team use a group chat?
3. What blocked the north road?
4. Which two options did Izzie give Rosa?
5. Why did Rosa prefer a later start?
6. Why could the team use only one jeep?
7. Which items did the team leave behind?
8. What new arrangement did Izzie post?
9. How did Rosa help the waiting families?
10. What did Izzie learn about teamwork?

### Creative Questions

1. Which message in the story was most useful? Why? Model: **The arrival-time message was useful because families knew when to wait.**
2. What information should you include when a plan changes?
3. Would you continue or cancel the event? Give one reason.
4. How can a group chat create confusion?
5. Write a friendly two-sentence delay message.

### Fun Activities

#### Activity 1: Message Sort

Put these messages in a useful order: **confirm safety; explain the problem; offer two options; confirm the decision; send the arrival time**. Then write one example for each step.

#### Activity 2: Plan B Meeting

Choose three things the team can take in one small vehicle: medicine, cool boxes, water, a large table, chairs, food, extra posters. Explain your choices. Several answers are valid if medicine stays safe.
