# Unit 14: Pronouns and Personal Connections

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Use **me, him, her, us, them** after verbs and prepositions.
- Use **mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs** without a following noun.
- Use common reflexive forms such as **myself** and **yourself**.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Work from the noun to the replacement. First underline the person or thing, then ask: **Does a noun follow? Is it receiving the action? Is the subject doing the action to the same person?** Use your answer to choose the pronoun.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Compare: *This is my coat. It is mine.* A possessive adjective has a noun; a possessive pronoun replaces it. Object pronouns follow verbs and prepositions: *Call her. Come with us.* Reflexive pronouns show that the subject and object are the same: *He taught himself.*

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Objektinis įvardis eina po veiksmažodžio ar prielinksnio: <strong>call her, with us</strong>. Savybinis įvardis vartojamas be daiktavardžio: <strong>It is mine</strong>. Sangrąžinis įvardis rodo, kad veikėjas veiksmą atlieka sau: <strong>He taught himself</strong>.</p>

### Pronoun map / Įvardžių lentelė

| Subject | Object | Possessive adjective + noun | Possessive pronoun | Reflexive |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | me | my bag | mine | myself |
| you | you | your bag | yours | yourself / yourselves |
| he | him | his bag | his | himself |
| she | her | her bag | hers | herself |
| it | it | its cover | — | itself |
| we | us | our bag | ours | ourselves |
| they | them | their bag | theirs | themselves |

Compare these three meanings:

- **Marta called me.** Marta called another person: me.
- **Marta called herself.** Marta called Marta.
- **That phone is hers.** The phone belongs to Marta.

**Lithuanian-speaker alert:** English normally keeps the object pronoun: **I see him every day.** Do not omit **him**, even when the person is clear from context.

### People and relationship language / Žodynėlis

**relative, colleague, flatmate, neighbour, classmate, partner, close friend, get on well, keep in touch, help each other, spend time together**

Make one true sentence with an object pronoun, one with a possessive pronoun, and one with a reflexive pronoun:

- **My neighbour often helps me.**
- **The garden is ours.**
- **I made this card myself.**

### Pronouns in a sentence / Įvardžiai sakinyje

Notice the position: **She** called **me**. I spoke to **her**. That coat is **hers**. She repaired it **herself**. **By myself** can also mean “alone”: *I live by myself.*

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Dažna klaida:</strong> lietuvių kalboje įvardį kartais galima numanyti, bet anglų kalboje jis dažnai būtinas: ne <strong>Called yesterday</strong>, o <strong>She called me yesterday</strong>. Formos <strong>mine, yours, hers</strong> vartojamos be daiktavardžio.</p>

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

In natural speech, object pronouns are usually not the strongest words. Stress the person or action that is new: **MARTA called me.** — **I called HER.** Read the pairs and change the stress to change the contrast. Keep **ours** as one syllable and pronounce the final **s** in **hers, ours, theirs**.

## Exercise 2: Choose the Correct Pronoun / Pratimas 2

1. This bicycle is **my / mine**.
2. I know Marta. I work with **she / her**.
3. We made the meal by **us / ourselves**.
4. Their flat is larger than **our / ours**.
5. Please send **me / mine** the photo.
6. Did you paint the room **yourself / yours**?

## Exercise 3: Rewrite with Pronouns / Pratimas 3

1. I called Lina, but Lina did not answer.
2. This is our table. That table is their table.
3. Jonas repaired the bicycle without help.
4. My parents invited my sister and me.

## Exercise 4: Read About a Shared Flat / Pratimas 4

**Read in two passes:** first identify who lives together; then draw arrows from **theirs, mine, us, herself, ourselves** to the people they refer to.

> I share a flat with Ugnė and Sam. The large bedroom is theirs, and the small one is mine. We divide the chores between us. Ugnė usually cooks, but yesterday she hurt herself, so Sam and I made dinner ourselves. Our neighbour brought us some soup too.

1. Who has the small bedroom?
2. Whose is the large bedroom?
3. Why did Ugnė not cook yesterday?
4. Who made dinner?

## Exercise 5: Correct the Mistakes / Pratimas 5

1. This phone is her. 2. Come with we. 3. I fixed it myselves. 4. Those bags are our.

## Exercise 6: Introduce Your People / Pratimas 6

Describe two people and something you share. Model: **My closest friends are Ieva and Ben. I see them every week. This photo is ours. We took it ourselves during a trip. I can always ask them for help.**

Write 6–8 connected sentences. You may describe real people or invent safe fictional details.

**Sentence starters:**
**The people I want to introduce are... / I met them... / I usually... with them. / This... is ours. / We made/took... ourselves. / I value them because...**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] I named two people and then used object pronouns to avoid repetition.
- [ ] I used a possessive pronoun without a noun after it.
- [ ] I used one reflexive pronoun with the correct subject.
- [ ] I connected at least two ideas with **and, but, because**, or **so**.

### One-minute connection talk / Vienos minutės pasakojimas

Speak from five keywords, not from a full script: **people – relationship – shared activity – shared object – reason**. Listen to yourself once. On the second attempt, replace repeated names with clear pronouns.

## Exercise 7: Follow the Reference / Pratimas 7

Choose the pronoun that clearly refers to the bold words.

1. **Milda** sent **Jonas and me** a message. _____ invited _____ to dinner. (**She/Her**; **we/us**)
2. I found **Tomas's keys**. I gave _____ back to _____. (**they/them**; **he/him**)
3. **My sister and I** made the cake. We made it by _____. (**ourselves/themselves**)
4. This is **Eglė's seat**. The seat is _____. (**her/hers**)
5. **You and Darius** can help _____. I cannot carry the table alone. (**me/mine**)
6. These tickets belong to **all of us**. They are _____. (**our/ours**)

## Exercise 8: A Birthday Photo / Pratimas 8

Read the conversation.

> **Gabija:** Is this photo yours, Leo?
> **Leo:** Yes, my cousin sent it to me. That's her beside the window.
> **Gabija:** And who are the two people behind her?
> **Leo:** They're her neighbours. She invited them to her birthday meal. We cooked everything ourselves.
> **Gabija:** The cake looks great! Did you make it?
> **Leo:** No, that was my cousin's work. The cake was hers; I only made the salad.

1. Who sent the photo to Leo?
2. Where is Leo's cousin in the photo?
3. Who did she invite?
4. Who prepared the food?
5. Which food did Leo make?
6. What does **hers** refer to in the final line?

## Exercise 9: Repair the Message / Pratimas 9

Replace each bold form with the correct pronoun.

> Hi Ana, **Me** am at the sports centre with Luka. Can you meet **we** at six? Luka has your water bottle, so ask **he** for it. The blue towel is **my**, but the grey one is **your**. We brought all the equipment **ourself**.

Write the six corrections in order.

## Exercise 10: Read, Cover, and Pass On the Message / Pratimas 10

Ask a partner to read the voice message twice, or read it aloud yourself. Cover it. Then complete the notes from memory.

> **Voice message:** Hi, it's Monika. I can't meet you at the café because my brother needs me at home. His car isn't working, so I'm taking him to work. Our cinema tickets are with Ema. Please call her and ask her to meet you outside the cinema at quarter to seven. She bought the tickets herself.

1. Monika cannot meet at the _____ .
2. Her _____ needs help.
3. She is taking him to _____ .
4. _____ has their cinema tickets.
5. The meeting place is _____ the cinema.
6. The meeting time is _____ .

Now retell the message using **her, him, our/their, hers/theirs** where possible.

## Exercise 11: Introduce and Connect People / Pratimas 11

Prepare a short spoken introduction to two real or imaginary people. Explain how you know them, what belongs to whom, and one thing somebody did without help.

Sentence starters: **This is... / I know him/her from... / This ... is his/hers. / We ... together. / He/She did ... himself/herself.**

Model: **This is my friend Saulius, and this is his sister Ieva. I know them from our running club. The red bicycle is his, and the helmet is hers. We planned this trip ourselves. Ieva repaired her bicycle herself.**

Success checklist:

- [ ] I used two object pronouns.
- [ ] I used a possessive adjective with a noun and a possessive pronoun without one.
- [ ] I used one reflexive pronoun correctly.
- [ ] My listener could identify every person and object.

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can avoid repeating names and nouns.
- [ ] I can explain ownership clearly.
- [ ] I can introduce people in a connected, respectful way.
- [ ] I can pass on a short message using clear pronoun references.
