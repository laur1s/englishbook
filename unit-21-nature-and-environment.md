# Unit 21: Nature and the Environment

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Discuss simple environmental problems and actions.
- Use the first conditional for likely future results.
- Use **too much, too many, enough, less, fewer**.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Describe one observable problem, connect it to a manageable action, and state a likely result without exaggeration. Finish with a small plan that can be checked after seven days.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **if + Present Simple, will + base verb**: *If we reuse bags, we will produce less waste.* Use **less** with uncountable nouns and **fewer** with plural countable nouns. **Too much/many** means more than needed; **enough** means sufficient.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Pirmojo tipo sąlygoje po <strong>if</strong> vartokite Present Simple, o rezultatui dažnai <strong>will + bendratis</strong>. <strong>Less</strong> vartojamas su neskaičiuojamais, <strong>fewer</strong> – su daugiskaitiniais skaičiuojamais daiktavardžiais.</p>

### From a Problem to a Practical Action / Nuo problemos iki praktiško veiksmo

| Problem | Everyday action | Possible result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| too much packaging | choose products with less packaging | produce less rubbish |
| food waste | plan meals and freeze extra food | throw away less food |
| air pollution | walk, cycle, or use public transport | make fewer car journeys |
| wasted energy | switch off lights and lower the heating | use less electricity |
| damaged natural areas | stay on paths and take litter home | protect plants and animals |

Use **because** to give a reason and **so** to give a result:

- *We take reusable bags because we want to avoid plastic.*
- *We want to avoid plastic, so we take reusable bags.*

Be realistic. One person cannot solve every environmental problem, but a person can describe a useful local action. Words such as **can, could, may, often**, and **usually** help you avoid claims that are too strong.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Svarbi pastaba:</strong> <em>less</em> vartojame su neskaičiuojamais žodžiais, pvz., <em>less water, less traffic, less packaging</em>. <em>Fewer</em> vartojame su suskaičiuojama daugiskaita: <em>fewer bottles, fewer journeys</em>. Kasdienėje kalboje galima išgirsti <em>less cars</em>, bet šiame kurse mokomės standartinės formos <em>fewer cars</em>.</p>

### First Conditional: Meaning and Form / Pirmasis sąlygos sakinys

The first conditional describes a possible future situation and its likely result:

> **If** the weather **is** safe, we **will clean** the riverbank.

The clauses can change order. Use a comma when the **if** clause comes first:

- *If I take a bottle, I won't need a plastic cup.*
- *I won't need a plastic cup if I take a bottle.*

You can also use an imperative in the result: *If the bin is full, take your rubbish home.* Do not use **will** after **if** in this pattern.

### Choose the quantity phrase / Pasirinkite kiekio frazę

| Noun type | More than needed | Smaller quantity | Sufficient |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| uncountable: water, traffic, waste | **too much water** | **less water** | **enough water** |
| plural countable: bottles, cars, bins | **too many bottles** | **fewer bottles** | **enough bottles** |

Put **enough** before a noun but after an adjective: **enough bins; the bin is large enough**. Compare **too much plastic** (an amount of material) and **too many plastic bags** (items we can count).

**Common repairs:**

- *fewer pollution* → **less pollution**
- *less car journeys* → **fewer car journeys**
- *enough clean* → **clean enough**

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Formos atrama:</strong> pirmiausia paklauskite, ar daiktavardį galima suskaičiuoti. Su daugiskaitiniais skaičiuojamais žodžiais vartokite <strong>many/fewer</strong>, su neskaičiuojamais – <strong>much/less</strong>. Po <strong>if</strong> šiame modelyje nevartokite <strong>will</strong>: <strong>If we walk, we will...</strong>, ne <strong>If we will walk...</strong>.</p>

### Worked example: observation to action

> **Observation:** Our office bin contains too many paper cups every afternoon.
>
> **Small action:** We can put washable cups beside the coffee machine and add a clear sign.
>
> **Likely result:** If more colleagues use them, we will throw away fewer paper cups and produce less waste.
>
> **Check:** After one week, we can count the paper cups again.

This example does not promise to “solve pollution.” It names a visible local problem, gives a manageable action, predicts a likely result, and includes a simple way to check progress.

### Three-part recall / Trijų dalių kartojimas

Cover the worked example and say **problem → action → likely result**. Stress the contrast words: **TOO MANY cups → FEWER cups; TOO MUCH waste → LESS waste.** Pause lightly after an opening **if** clause: **If we reuse the cups, | we will produce less waste.** Then create one new chain about water, transport, food, or energy.

## Exercise 2: Complete the Conditional / Pratimas 2

1. If we _____ (walk), we will reduce pollution.
2. The park will look better if people _____ (not drop) litter.
3. If I remember my bottle, I _____ (not buy) a plastic one.
4. What _____ you do if the bin is full?
5. If shops use less packaging, there _____ (be) less waste.
6. We _____ (save) energy if we turn off the lights.

## Exercise 4: Read a Community Notice / Pratimas 4

> River Clean-up: Meet at the footbridge on Sunday at 10 a.m. We will provide gloves and rubbish bags, so you do not need to bring them. Wear strong shoes and bring enough water. If the weather is dangerous, we will move the event to the following Sunday. Children under 14 must come with an adult.

1. Where and when will volunteers meet?
2. Which equipment is provided?
3. What should volunteers bring?
4. What will happen if the weather is dangerous?

## Exercise 5: Decide Which Action Helps More / Pratimas 5

Compare two actions: reusable bottle vs recycling one bottle; walking one short journey vs buying a new electric car. Use **because, less, fewer**, and one conditional.

There is no single correct choice; compare **effort, cost, frequency,** and **likely effect**. Model: **Carrying a reusable bottle is a better first step for me because it costs little and I can do it every day. If I remember it, I will buy fewer plastic bottles and create less waste.**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] I clearly chose one action in each pair.
- [ ] My reasons use information I can justify.
- [ ] I used **less** with an uncountable noun and **fewer** with a plural noun.
- [ ] My conditional describes a possible result, not a guaranteed global change.

## Exercise 6: Make a Seven-Day Eco Plan / Pratimas 6

Choose three realistic actions. Model: **I will carry a reusable bottle, so I will buy fewer plastic bottles. If the weather is good, I will cycle to work. I will plan two meals before shopping because I want to waste less food.**

Add when and where you will do each action. At the end of the week, mark each action **done**, **partly done**, or **not done yet**. Write one neutral reflection: *Cycling was difficult when it rained, so next week I will use the bus.*

## Exercise 7: Read a Building Notice and Two Replies / Pratimas 7

> **NOTICE: A cleaner shared courtyard**
> Our building produces too much mixed rubbish. From 1 May, there will be separate bins for paper, glass, food, and general waste. Please wash food containers quickly before recycling them. If an item is very dirty, put it in general waste. The new food bin will reduce the amount of rubbish that we send to landfill. On Saturday at 11, volunteers will also plant six small trees. Meet beside the bicycle shed and bring gardening gloves if you have them.
>
> **Jonas:** I can help on Saturday, but I don't have gloves. Do I need to buy some?
> **Sara:** I can't plant trees, but I can make a simple recycling guide for our neighbours.

1. What new bins will residents have?
2. What should residents do before they recycle food containers?
3. Where should a very dirty container go?
4. What will the food bin reduce?
5. Where and when will volunteers meet?
6. Which useful alternative does Sara offer?

## Exercise 8: Correct the Environmental Advice / Pratimas 8

Correct one error in each sentence.

1. If everyone will bring a cup, we will use fewer plastic cups.
2. There are too much cars outside the school.
3. We need fewer water when we take short showers.
4. If the weather is dangerous, the event move indoors.
5. Buy only enough food because you will make fewer food waste.
6. If you will see litter, put it in a bin.

## Exercise 9: Propose One Local Improvement / Pratimas 9

Choose a real or imagined place: your building, workplace, street, school, or local park. Write 80–100 words with:

- one clear problem supported by an observation;
- two realistic actions for different people;
- one first conditional;
- **less** and **fewer** used accurately;
- a polite invitation to help.

**Model:** *There are too many single-use cups in our office kitchen. We could put ordinary cups beside the coffee machine and add a clear washing sign. If more people use these cups, we will throw away fewer paper cups and create less waste. Would you like to help me make the sign?*

Check that your proposal does not blame one person and does not promise an impossible result.
