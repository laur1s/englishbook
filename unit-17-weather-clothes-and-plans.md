# Unit 17: Weather, Clothes, and Plans

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Understand a simple forecast and choose suitable clothes.
- Use Present Continuous for a fixed personal arrangement.
- Connect reasons and results with **because** and **so**.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Read a forecast for **time, weather, temperature, and change**. Then make an arrangement, choose suitable clothes, and prepare one realistic backup. Your final plan should answer: **when, where, with whom, what weather, what to wear, and what if conditions change?**

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use Present Continuous for an arrangement with a time or place: *I'm meeting Ema at six.* Use **going to** for a plan or evidence-based prediction: *Those clouds are dark. It's going to rain.* **Because** introduces a reason; **so** introduces a result.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> Present Continuous su laiku ar vieta gali reikšti sutartą planą. <strong>Going to</strong> tinka planui arba prognozei, kai matome požymius. <strong>Because</strong> pateikia priežastį, o <strong>so</strong> – rezultatą.</p>

### Three future messages / Trys ateities reikšmės

| Meaning | Useful form | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| personal arrangement already organised | Present Continuous | **We're meeting** at the station at nine. |
| intention or plan | **be going to** | **We're going to walk** by the lake. |
| prediction based on present evidence | **be going to** | Look at those clouds. **It's going to rain.** |

**Because** and **so** connect the same situation from different directions:

- I am taking boots **because** the path is wet. *(result + reason)*
- The path is wet, **so** I am taking boots. *(reason + result)*

Do not join both in one sentence: not *Because the path is wet, so I am taking boots.*

### Plan and temperature language / Plano ir temperatūros kalba

Plan language: *We're meeting... / I'm bringing... / If..., we'll... / Let's check again in the morning.*

Read these temperatures aloud: **14°C — fourteen degrees; −3°C — minus three degrees; 8–12°C — eight to twelve degrees**.

### Forecast and Clothing Bank / Prognozės ir drabužių žodynas

| Forecast phrase | Practical choice | Lithuanian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| light / heavy rain | an umbrella, a waterproof jacket | silpnas / stiprus lietus |
| strong winds | a windproof jacket | stiprus vėjas; neperpučiama striukė |
| temperatures below zero | a warm coat, gloves, a hat | temperatūra žemiau nulio |
| bright and sunny | sunglasses, sun cream | giedra ir saulėta |
| changeable weather | layers, a light jacket | permainingi orai; keli drabužių sluoksniai |

Useful forecast language: **It will be around 12°C. Rain is likely/possible. It will stay dry. The wind will get stronger.** Useful arrangement questions: **What time are we meeting? Where are we meeting? What are you bringing?**

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Kalbos pastaba:</strong> sakome <strong>It is sunny/windy/cloudy</strong>, bet <strong>It is raining/snowing</strong>. Temperatūrai tinka <strong>It is 10 degrees</strong> arba <strong>The temperature is 10°C</strong>.</p>

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

In conversation, contractions are natural: **I'm meeting**, **we're leaving**, **it's going to rain**. Keep the **-ing** ending clear in **meeting, leaving, raining**. Practise the rhythm: **We're MEETing at NINE. / It's GOing to RAIN.**

## Exercise 4: Read the Weekend Forecast / Pratimas 4

> Saturday morning will be dry and bright, but strong winds will arrive after lunch. The temperature will fall from 14°C to 8°C. Sunday will start cloudy, and heavy rain is likely in the afternoon. The hiking group is meeting at 9 a.m. on Saturday, so everyone should bring a windproof jacket.

2. How much will the temperature fall?
3. When is heavy rain likely?
4. What should hikers bring?

## Exercise 5: Solve the Packing Problem / Pratimas 5

Choose three useful items for the forecast: **sandals, windproof jacket, umbrella, thick winter coat, water bottle**. More than one choice can be sensible, so explain each answer with **because** or **so**.

## Exercise 6: Plan an Outdoor Day / Pratimas 6

Write your arrangement, forecast, clothes, and backup plan. Model: **We are meeting at the lake at ten. It is going to be warm in the morning, so I am wearing light clothes. Rain is possible later, so I am taking a jacket. If it rains heavily, we will visit the museum.**

Write 7–9 sentences, then present the plan in about one minute.

**Sentence starters:**
**We are meeting... at... / The forecast says... / I am taking... because... / It may..., so... / If the weather changes, we will...**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] My arrangement includes a specific time and place.
- [ ] I separated an arrangement from a weather prediction.
- [ ] I used **because** for a reason and **so** for a result.
- [ ] My clothes match the stated weather.
- [ ] My backup plan is possible in the stated conditions.

### Forecast update / Orų prognozės pasikeitimas

After you finish, respond without rewriting everything: **The temperature is now five degrees lower and the outdoor place closes at noon.** Say what stays the same and what you will change.

Useful response: **We're still meeting at nine, but I'm bringing a warmer layer. Because the park closes early, we'll go there first.**

## Exercise 7: Connect Reason and Result / Pratimas 7

Complete with **because** or **so**.

1. The path may be wet, _____ I am wearing boots.
2. I am taking gloves _____ the temperature will be below zero.
3. It is going to be sunny, _____ remember your sunglasses.
4. We moved the picnic indoors _____ strong winds are likely.
5. The weather is changeable, _____ I am wearing layers.
6. I am bringing a waterproof jacket _____ heavy rain is possible.

Then rewrite one sentence by reversing the order: **I am wearing boots because the path may be wet.**

## Exercise 8: Messages About a Day Trip / Pratimas 8

Read the group chat.

> **Aistė, 18:10:** Are we still meeting outside the station at 8:30 tomorrow?
> **Ben, 18:12:** Yes. The train is leaving at 8:45, so please be on time.
> **Karolis, 18:15:** The morning will be sunny, but rain is likely after two. I'm bringing a light waterproof jacket.
> **Aistė, 18:17:** Good idea. I'm wearing walking shoes because the forest path may be wet.
> **Ben, 18:20:** If the rain starts early, we'll visit the castle instead of walking by the lake.

1. Where and when are they meeting?
2. What time does the train leave?
3. When is rain likely?
4. What is Karolis bringing?
5. Why is Aistė wearing walking shoes?
6. What is their backup plan?

## Exercise 9: Choose the Best Future Form / Pratimas 9

Choose the best option.

1. We have arranged it: we **are meeting / will perhaps meet** at the bus stop at seven.
2. Look at those dark clouds! It **is going to rain / is raining tomorrow at three**.
3. The forecast says the wind **will get / will getting** stronger tonight.
4. I have a dentist appointment: I **am seeing / am see** the dentist tomorrow.
5. We bought our tickets, so we **are flying / are fly** on Monday.
6. I think tomorrow **will be / is meeting** warmer.

## Exercise 10: Read, Cover, and Remember the Forecast / Pratimas 10

Ask a partner to read the forecast twice, or read it aloud yourself. Cover it before answering.

> **Local forecast:** Friday morning will be cloudy with light rain and a temperature of 9°C. It will become dry around midday. Strong winds will arrive from the west in the evening. Saturday will be brighter and warmer, with a high of 16°C, but showers are possible after five.

1. What will Friday morning be like?
2. What will the temperature be?
3. When will it become dry?
4. What will arrive in the evening?
5. Which day will be warmer?
6. When are showers possible on Saturday?

Retell the forecast in three sentences without looking at it.

## Exercise 11: Give a Weather and Plan Update / Pratimas 11

Give a 1–2 minute update for a real or imaginary day out. Include the meeting arrangement, forecast, suitable clothes, and a backup plan.

Sentence starters: **We're meeting... / The forecast says... / I'm bringing... because... / If..., we'll...**

Model: **We're meeting at the station at nine. The forecast says it will be cool and windy, so I'm wearing a warm jacket. I'm bringing an umbrella because showers are possible. If it rains heavily, we'll go to a café and visit the gallery.**

Success checklist:

- [ ] I gave a clear time and place arrangement.
- [ ] I used at least two weather phrases.
- [ ] I linked a clothing choice to a reason.
- [ ] I gave a realistic backup plan with **if**.
