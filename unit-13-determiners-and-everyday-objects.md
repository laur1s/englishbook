# Unit 13: Determiners and Everyday Objects

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Choose **a/an, the**, or no article in everyday contexts.
- Point to things with **this, that, these, those**.
- Avoid repetition with **one/ones**.
- Describe and identify lost property.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

1. Read the examples and point to real objects as you say **this, that, these, those**.
2. Complete Exercises 1–3 without looking back, then check the rule you missed.
3. Read the lost-property notice twice: first for the main idea, then for details.
4. Finish by producing a description that another person could genuinely use.

**Success today:** you can introduce an object, refer to it again, and distinguish it from a similar object.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **a/an** for one thing not identified before: *I found a key.* Use **the** when the listener can identify it: *The key is on the desk.* Use no article for general plurals: *Keys are easy to lose.* **This/these** usually point to things near us; **that/those** usually point to things farther away. Use **one/ones** after an adjective: *the blue one; the small ones*.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> <strong>a/an</strong> pristato vieną dar neapibrėžtą daiktą, o <strong>the</strong> nurodo konkretų, pašnekovui atpažįstamą daiktą. <strong>This/these</strong> dažniausiai rodo arti, <strong>that/those</strong> – toliau. <strong>One/ones</strong> leidžia nekartoti daiktavardžio.</p>

### See the pattern / Pastebėkite modelį

| Meaning | Form | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| one new, non-specific thing | **a/an + singular noun** | I need **a** cable. Is there **an** adapter here? |
| a known or clearly identified thing | **the + noun** | Where is **the cable from my bag**? |
| things in general | **plural noun, no article** | **Cables** break easily. |
| near the speaker | **this / these** | **This receipt** is mine. **These keys** are yours. |
| farther from the speaker | **that / those** | Is **that coat** yours? Are **those gloves** wet? |
| replace a repeated noun | **one / ones** | I prefer the small **one**. I need the black **ones**. |

**Important:** use **an** before a vowel sound, not simply a vowel letter: *an umbrella, an hour*, but *a university*.

### Object categories and locations / Daiktų grupės ir vieta

- **Personal things:** wallet, purse, keys, glasses, headphones, umbrella
- **Technology:** charger, cable, adapter, power bank, case
- **Location phrases:** on the shelf, under the seat, beside the till, inside the pocket

Say three chains aloud:

- **I found a bag. The bag is black. It is the small one by the door.**
- **Are these your keys? No, mine are the silver ones on the desk.**
- **I need an adapter. Do you have one?**

### Common repair / Dažna klaida

Do not say *I like the blue* when a noun is understood. Say **I like the blue one**. Do not use **one** after a possessive pronoun: say **This one is mine**, not *This one is my one*.

### Useful Vocabulary and Chunks / Naudingi žodžiai ir junginiai

| Object | Useful description | Lithuanian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| a wallet | brown leather, with a zip | piniginė; ruda odinė, su užtrauktuku |
| a charger | white, with a short cable | įkroviklis; baltas, su trumpu laidu |
| an umbrella | dark blue, with a wooden handle | skėtis; tamsiai mėlynas, medine rankena |
| a backpack | medium-sized, with two pockets | kuprinė; vidutinio dydžio, su dviem kišenėmis |
| a pair of gloves | black wool, size M | pirštinių pora; juodos vilnonės, M dydžio |

Useful questions: **Which one is yours? Is this the one? What does it look like? Where did you last see it?** Learn colour and material as a chunk: **a black leather wallet**, not *a leather black wallet*.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Kalbos pastaba:</strong> keli įprasti būdvardžiai prieš daiktavardį dažniausiai eina tokia tvarka: dydis + spalva + medžiaga + daiktas: <strong>a small black leather bag</strong>. A2 lygiu svarbiausia įsiminti dažnus junginius, o ne ilgą taisyklę.</p>

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

In **this, that, these, those**, put your tongue lightly between your teeth for **th**. Your voice is on. Contrast **these keys** (plural, near) with **this key** (singular, near). Read each chunk slowly, then at natural speed: **this one — that one — these ones — those ones**.

## Exercise 2: Choose the Determiner / Pratimas 2

1. I need **a / the** charger, but any charger is fine.
2. Where is **a / the** charger I left here?
3. **This / These** keys in my hand are mine.
4. Who owns **that / those** coat across the room?
5. I like the black shoes, not the brown **one / ones**.
6. **Wallets / The wallets** often contain bank cards. (general meaning)

## Exercise 3: Put the Words in Order / Pratimas 3

1. one / cheaper / the / is / blue
2. Make a question: these / yours / gloves / are
3. umbrella / found / an / I
4. desk / the / umbrella / under / is / the

## Exercise 4: Read a Lost-Property Notice / Pratimas 4

**Reading strategy:** circle the identifying details: **where, when, colour, contents**. These details help an owner prove the item is theirs.

> I found a small black backpack on the 8:15 bus to Kaunas. The bag has a green notebook, two chargers, and a pair of glasses inside. It does not have a name on it. Ask at the station office and describe one other item in the bag.

1. Where was the backpack found?
2. What colour is it?
3. Name two things inside it.
4. Why must the owner describe another item?

## Exercise 5: Complete the Dialogue / Pratimas 5

Use **one, those, the, this**.

A: Is _____ grey scarf yours? B: No, mine is _____ blue _____. A: What about _____ gloves by the door? B: Yes, they are mine. Thanks!

## Exercise 6: Describe a Lost Item / Pratimas 6

Write 5–6 sentences. Include colour, size, material, contents, and where you last saw it. Model: **I lost a small brown wallet. The wallet has two cards and a bus ticket in it. I last saw it in this café. It is the leather one with a silver button. Please call me if you find it.**

**Sentence starters:**
**I lost a/an... / It is... / It has... inside. / I last saw it... / It is the... one. / Please...**

**Success checklist:**

- [ ] I used **a/an** to introduce the item and **the** when I mentioned it again.
- [ ] I gave at least three details that distinguish it from similar objects.
- [ ] I said where or when I last saw it.
- [ ] I ended with a clear request or contact instruction.

### Speak and respond / Kalbėkite ir reaguokite

Read your description aloud without showing the title. A partner—or you on a second reading—asks two questions:

- **Is it this one or that one?**
- **What is inside it?**
- **Where exactly did you leave it?**

Answer in full sentences. If a detail is missing, add it to your written description.

## Exercise 8: At the Lost-Property Office / Pratimas 8

Read the dialogue.

> **Assistant:** Good afternoon. How can I help?
> **Rūta:** I think I left a pair of gloves on the train.
> **Assistant:** What do they look like?
> **Rūta:** They are grey wool gloves with small blue buttons.
> **Assistant:** Are those gloves on the shelf yours?
> **Rūta:** No, the dark ones are not mine. Mine are lighter.
> **Assistant:** What about these ones in the box?
> **Rūta:** Yes, those are the ones. Thank you!

Answer the questions.

1. Where did Rūta probably leave her gloves?
2. What material are they?
3. Which detail helps identify them?
4. Why does Rūta reject the gloves on the shelf?
5. Which phrase does the assistant use to point to the gloves nearby?

## Exercise 10: Read, Cover, and Remember / Pratimas 10

This is a text-based listening activity. Ask a partner to read the message twice, or read it aloud yourself. Do not write while you listen/read. Then cover the message and answer from memory.

> **Station message:** We found a medium-sized green suitcase on platform four at 17:20. It has a yellow ribbon on the handle. The owner should go to Office 2 and bring an ID card. The office closes at eight o'clock.

1. What item was found?
2. Where and when was it found?
3. What special detail is on it?
4. Which office should the owner visit?
5. What should the owner bring?
6. When does the office close?

Check the text only after answering. Which details were easiest to remember: the object, numbers, or colours?

## Exercise 11: Lost-Item Role-Play / Pratimas 11

Student A is a traveller; Student B works at lost property. Speak for 1–2 minutes, then change roles.

**Traveller starters:** *I’m looking for... / I last saw it... / It’s the ... one with...*
**Assistant starters:** *What does it look like? / Is this the one? / What is inside it?*

Model: **A: I’m looking for a small black backpack. B: Is that one by the door yours? A: No, mine has two green pockets. B: What is inside it? A: A notebook and a charger.**

Success checklist:

- [ ] I used at least one article and one demonstrative accurately.
- [ ] I gave three identifying details.
- [ ] I used **one/ones** instead of repeating a noun at least once.
- [ ] I asked or answered a checking question.

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can identify an object clearly without repeating the same noun.
- [ ] I can write a useful lost-property description.
- [ ] I can ask a follow-up question when a description is not clear enough.
- [ ] I can understand and give key details at a lost-property office.
