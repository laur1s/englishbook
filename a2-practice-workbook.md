# A2 Everyday Practice Workbook
## A2 kasdienės praktikos pratybos

This workbook gives you **24 substantial practice sets**—one for every course unit. Each set combines grammar, vocabulary, reading, writing, and speaking. / Šiose pratybose yra **24 išsamūs praktikos rinkiniai**—po vieną kiekvienam kurso skyriui. Kiekviename rinkinyje derinama gramatika, žodynas, skaitymas, rašymas ir kalbėjimas.

### A simple weekly rhythm / Paprastas savaitės ritmas

1. Do one set without looking at the key. / Atlikite vieną rinkinį nežiūrėdami į atsakymus.
2. Check the fixed answers and correct your work in another colour. / Patikrinkite uždarų užduočių atsakymus ir pataisykite kitu rašikliu.
3. Read the model aloud twice. / Du kartus garsiai perskaitykite pavyzdį.
4. Record your speaking answer for 30–60 seconds. / Įrašykite 30–60 sekundžių kalbėjimo atsakymą.
5. Repeat difficult sentences two days later. / Po dviejų dienų pakartokite sudėtingus sakinius.

Open tasks are not automatically graded. Compare your work with the model and checklist. / Atviros užduotys automatiškai nevertinamos. Palyginkite savo darbą su pavyzdžiu ir kontroliniu sąrašu.

### How to understand and repair a mistake / Kaip suprasti ir ištaisyti klaidą

Do not only copy the correct answer. Mark the reason, write the corrected sentence, and make one new sentence with the same pattern. / Neužtenka tik nusirašyti teisingą atsakymą. Pažymėkite klaidos priežastį, perrašykite taisyklingą sakinį ir sukurkite vieną naują sakinį pagal tą patį modelį.

| Code | What to check | Ką tikrinti | Repair example / Taisymo pavyzdys |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VF** | verb form | veiksmažodžio forma | *She work* → *She works* |
| **T** | time/tense | laikas ir veiksmažodžio laikas | *I have seen it yesterday* → *I saw it yesterday* |
| **WO** | word order | žodžių tvarka | *Where you are?* → *Where are you?* |
| **DET** | article/determiner | artikelis arba pažymimasis žodis | *I need umbrella* → *I need an umbrella* |
| **PREP** | preposition | prielinksnis | *in Monday* → *on Monday* |
| **Q** | quantity word | kiekio žodis | *much cars* → *many cars* |
| **REG** | tone/register | mandagumas ir stilius | *Give me...* → *Could I have..., please?* |

**Three-step repair / Trijų žingsnių taisymas:** 1. Circle the problem / Apibraukite klaidą. 2. Say the reason in Lithuanian / Lietuviškai pasakykite priežastį. 3. Make a personal example / Sukurkite asmeninį pavyzdį.

## Exercise 1 — Meeting a New Neighbour

> **LT pagalba:** Rinkitės **am** su *I*, **is** su *he/she/it*, **are** su *you/we/they*. Klausime veiksmažodis eina prieš asmenį: *Are you...?* Jei suklydote, pažymėkite **VF** arba **WO**.

### A. Complete the dialogue with **am, is, are, isn't,** or **aren't**. / Užpildykite dialogą tinkama veiksmažodžio **to be** forma.

> **Marta:** Hi! I ___ Marta. ___ you new here?  
> **Jonas:** Yes, I ___. My flat ___ on the second floor.  
> **Marta:** Nice to meet you. I ___ in flat 7. This ___ my dog, Pip.  
> **Jonas:** He ___ very big!  
> **Marta:** No, he ___. He is only six months old.

### B. Put the words in order. / Sudėkite žodžius taisyklinga tvarka.

1. name / your / what / is / ?
2. from / you / are / where / ?
3. meet / nice / you / to
4. not / I / Vilnius / from / am

### C. Personal response / Asmeninis atsakymas

Write and say a four-sentence introduction. Include your name, town, work or study, and one friendly question. / Parašykite ir garsiai pasakykite keturių sakinių prisistatymą: vardas, miestas, darbas arba mokslai ir vienas draugiškas klausimas.

**Starter:** *Hello, I'm ... I live in ...*  
**Model:** *Hello, I'm Rūta. I live in Kaunas and I work in a bookshop. I enjoy quiet cafés. What do you like doing at weekends?*  
**Checklist:** four complete sentences; **am/is/are** used correctly; one question; clear voice.

## Exercise 2 — A Realistic Daily Routine

> **LT pagalba:** Dažnumo prieveiksmis dažniausiai eina prieš pagrindinį veiksmažodį (*I usually work*), bet po **to be** (*I am usually home*). Su *he/she/it* pridėkite **-s**. Klaidos kodai: **VF**, **PREP**.

### A. Choose the best frequency expression. / Pasirinkite tinkamiausią dažnumo posakį.

1. I brush my teeth **once / every** morning.
2. We go swimming **twice a week / twice week**.
3. Tomas is **usually / usual** home by six.
4. I **hardly ever / hardly never** take a taxi.
5. My class starts **at / on** 18:30.
6. We visit my parents **in / at** Sundays.

### B. Complete with the Present Simple form. / Įrašykite taisyklingą Present Simple formą.

1. Eglė ___ (finish) work at five.
2. Her husband ___ (cook) on weekdays.
3. They ___ (not watch) television during dinner.
4. ___ their son ___ (walk) to school?
5. I ___ (have) lunch at my desk on busy days.
6. My colleagues ___ (go) home by bus.

### C. Routine upgrade

Write five sentences about a normal weekday and one sentence about a habit you want to change. / Parašykite penkis sakinius apie įprastą darbo dieną ir vieną sakinį apie įprotį, kurį norite pakeisti.

**Model:** *I usually get up at seven. I make coffee and check my calendar. I start work at nine. I sometimes walk at lunchtime. In the evening, I cook with my partner. I want to use my phone less before bed.*  
**Checklist:** one frequency adverb; two time expressions; third-person **-s** where needed; six sentences.

## Exercise 3 — The Missing Bag

> **LT pagalba:** Baigtam praeities įvykiui vartokite Past Simple. Netaisyklingas formas (*leave–left, go–went, feel–felt*) mokykitės poromis. Pasakojimą aiškina *first, then, after that, in the end*. Klaidos kodas: **T**.

### A. Complete the story with the Past Simple. / Užpildykite pasakojimą Past Simple formomis.

Yesterday Lina ___ (leave) her bag on a bus. She ___ (notice) it at the next stop and ___ (call) the bus company. An employee ___ (find) the bag under a seat. Lina ___ (go) to the station, ___ (show) her ID, and ___ (get) the bag back. She ___ (feel) very relieved.

### B. Choose the correct time connector. / Pasirinkite tinkamą įvykių sekos jungtuką.

1. **First / Finally**, Lina got on the bus.
2. **Then / Yesterday**, she sat near the window.
3. She got off, and **after that / last week** she noticed the missing bag.
4. **In the end / At first**, an employee returned it.

### C. Tell a small problem story

Write 6–8 sentences about a time when you lost, forgot, or broke something. / Parašykite 6–8 sakinius apie kartą, kai ką nors pametėte, pamiršote arba sugadinote.

**Starter:** *Last ..., I ... First, ... Then, ...*  
**Model:** *Last month, I forgot my keys at work. First, I called a colleague, but she was already on the bus. Then I waited in a café. My colleague came back after twenty minutes. She opened the office, and I found the keys on my desk. In the end, we laughed about it.*  
**Checklist:** clear beginning and ending; at least four past verbs; two connectors; understandable sequence.

## Exercise 4 — Shopping for Dinner

> **LT pagalba:** **Many** vartojamas su suskaičiuojamais daiktais (*many apples*), **much**—su nesuskaičiuojamais (*much milk*). **Some** dažnas teiginiuose, **any**—klausimuose ir neiginiuose. Klaidos kodai: **DET**, **Q**.

### A. Choose **a, an, some, any, much,** or **many**. / Pasirinkite tinkamą artikelį arba kiekio žodį.

1. We need ___ onion and ___ tomatoes.
2. Is there ___ rice at home?
3. There isn't ___ milk.
4. How ___ apples should I buy?
5. How ___ cheese do we need?
6. Please get ___ loaf of bread.

### B. Match the shop phrase to its purpose. / Susiekite frazę su jos paskirtimi parduotuvėje.

1. *Could I have half a kilo, please?*  
2. *Do you have this in a smaller size?*  
3. *Can I pay by card?*  
4. *I'm just looking, thank you.*

a. refusing help politely  
b. asking about payment  
c. asking for a quantity  
d. asking for another option

### C. Make a dinner shopping list

Plan a simple dinner for two. Write six items with quantities, then practise asking for three of them.

**Model:** *two chicken breasts, 500 grams of potatoes, a bag of salad, one lemon, some yoghurt, a bottle of water. Could I have 500 grams of potatoes, please?*  
**Checklist:** six items; suitable quantity words; three polite requests; countable and uncountable nouns checked.

## Exercise 5 — Finding the Library

> **LT pagalba:** Krypties veiksmai paprastai prasideda liepiamąja forma: *go, walk, turn, cross*. Vietą nusako *next to, between, across from*. Mandagų klausimą pradėkite *Excuse me, could you...?* Klaidos kodai: **PREP**, **REG**.

### A. Complete with **across from, between, next to, past, straight,** or **left**. / Įrašykite tinkamą krypties arba vietos žodį.

1. The library is ___ the bank and the pharmacy.
2. The café is ___ the cinema.
3. Go ___ for two blocks.
4. Walk ___ the supermarket.
5. Turn ___ at the traffic lights.
6. The bus stop is ___ the post office.

### B. Choose the more natural request. / Pasirinkite natūralesnį ir mandagesnį klausimą.

1. **Where library? / Excuse me, how can I get to the library?**
2. **Tell me bus station. / Could you tell me where the bus station is?**
3. **Is it far from here? / It far?**
4. **Thank you for your help. / Thank for help.**

### C. Give directions

Imagine a visitor is at your nearest bus stop. Give 5–7 steps to a useful place. / Įsivaizduokite, kad svečias yra artimiausioje autobusų stotelėje. 5–7 žingsniais paaiškinkite kelią į naudingą vietą.

**Model:** *Go straight for about 200 metres. Walk past the bakery and turn right at the bank. Cross the road carefully. The clinic is next to the flower shop, opposite the car park.*  
**Checklist:** starting point and destination; at least three direction verbs; two place prepositions; distances kept simple.

## Exercise 6 — Weekend Plans and Predictions

> **LT pagalba:** Susitarimui su konkrečiu laiku tinka Present Continuous; matomam požymiui ar ketinimui—**going to**; spontaniškam sprendimui ar nuomonei apie ateitį—**will**. Klaidos kodas: **T**.

### A. Choose **going to, will,** or the Present Continuous. / Pasirinkite ateities formą pagal kontekstą.

1. We ___ (meet) Ieva at 10:00 on Saturday; it is in my calendar.
2. Look at those clouds! It ___ (rain).
3. I think the new café ___ (be) popular.
4. The phone is ringing. I ___ (answer) it.
5. They bought paint yesterday. They ___ (decorate) the kitchen.
6. I ___ (see) the dentist tomorrow at three.

### B. Respond naturally.

1. *I can't carry both boxes.* — *I ___ help you.*
2. *What are your plans for Sunday?* — *I ___ visit my aunt.*
3. *Why are you taking an umbrella?* — *It ___ rain.*

### C. Make a flexible weekend plan

Write three arrangements, one intention, one prediction, and a backup plan. / Parašykite tris susitarimus, vieną ketinimą, vieną prognozę ir atsarginį planą.

**Model:** *I'm meeting Gabija on Saturday morning. We're having lunch at one. On Sunday, I'm visiting my parents. I'm going to make a cake. I think the weather will be warm. If it rains, we'll play board games inside.*  
**Checklist:** three future forms; times included for arrangements; one **if** backup; six sentences.

## Exercise 7 — Life Experience Interview

> **LT pagalba:** Present Perfect sieja praeitį su dabartimi ir dažnai eina su *ever, never, already, yet, for, since*. Jei nurodytas baigtas laikas (*last year, in 2024*), rinkitės Past Simple. Klaidos kodas: **T**.

### A. Complete with the Present Perfect. / Įrašykite Present Perfect formą.

1. ___ you ever ___ (travel) alone?
2. I ___ never ___ (try) surfing.
3. Mantas ___ already ___ (finish) the report.
4. We ___ not ___ (visit) that museum yet.
5. She ___ (live) here for three years.
6. They ___ (know) each other since 2022.

### B. Choose Present Perfect or Past Simple. / Pasirinkite Present Perfect arba Past Simple pagal laiko reikšmę.

1. I **have seen / saw** that film last Friday.
2. She **has visited / visited** Rome three times.
3. **Have you eaten / Did you eat** breakfast yet?
4. We **have moved / moved** here in 2021.
5. He **has just called / just called**; he is waiting outside now.

### C. Interview and follow up

Ask someone three **Have you ever ...?** questions. After each *yes*, ask **When? Where? Who with?** or **How was it?**

**Model:** *Have you ever slept in a tent? — Yes, I have. — Where did you camp? — Near a lake in Molėtai. It was cold but beautiful.*  
**Checklist:** three experience questions; at least two Past Simple follow-ups; short notes; report one answer afterwards.

## Exercise 8 — Choosing a Place to Stay

> **LT pagalba:** Trumpiems būdvardžiams dažniausiai pridėkite **-er/-est**; ilgesniems vartokite **more/most**. Po palyginamosios formos vartokite **than**. Netaisyklingos formos: *good–better–best*. Klaidos kodas: **VF**.

### A. Complete with the comparative or superlative form. / Įrašykite aukštesnįjį arba aukščiausiąjį būdvardžio laipsnį.

1. Hotel A is ___ (cheap) than Hotel B.
2. The guesthouse is the ___ (quiet) option.
3. Hotel B has ___ (comfortable) beds than Hotel A.
4. The hostel is ___ (far) from the centre.
5. This room is not as ___ (bright) as that one.
6. Which place has the ___ (good) reviews?

### B. Compare the facts.

| Place | Price | Centre | Rating |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Pine Hotel | €85 | 1 km | 8.8 |
| River Hostel | €42 | 3 km | 8.1 |
| Old Town Rooms | €70 | 0.4 km | 9.2 |

Write three correct comparison sentences. / Parašykite tris taisyklingus palyginimo sakinius pagal lentelę.

### C. Recommend one place

**Model:** *I recommend Old Town Rooms. It is more expensive than the hostel, but it is closer to the centre. It also has the best rating. It is the most suitable choice for a short city break.*  
**Checklist:** recommendation stated; two comparisons; one superlative; reason connected with **but, because,** or **also**.

## Exercise 9 — Advice, Rules, and Possibility

> **LT pagalba:** **Should**—patarimas, **must/have to**—pareiga, **mustn't**—draudimas, **don't have to**—nebūtina, **might**—galimybė. *Mustn't* ir *don't have to* nėra tas pats. Klaidos kodai: **VF**, **REG**.

### A. Choose the best modal. / Pasirinkite modalinį veiksmažodį pagal reikšmę.

1. You **should / mustn't** rest if you feel tired.
2. Visitors **must / might** show ID at reception; it is a building rule.
3. You **mustn't / don't have to** park here; it is forbidden.
4. We **might / have to** go for a walk later, but we are not sure.
5. You **don't have to / mustn't** bring food; I have cooked enough.
6. I **can / should** speak Lithuanian and English.

### B. Rewrite politely. / Perrašykite sakinius mandagiau.

1. *Give me your charger.* → Could ...?
2. *Open the window.* → Would you mind ...?
3. *Don't talk loudly here.* → You shouldn't ...

### C. Give balanced advice

Situation: *Your friend works late, sleeps badly, and feels stressed.* Give three suggestions, one thing to avoid, and one question. / Situacija: draugas vėlai dirba, prastai miega ir jaučia įtampą. Pateikite tris pasiūlymus, vieną dalyką, kurio geriau vengti, ir vieną klausimą.

**Model:** *You should take a short break after work. You could put your phone away before bed. It might help to go for a walk. You shouldn't drink coffee late in the evening. Would you like to exercise together this weekend?*  
**Checklist:** advice, not diagnosis; three different modal forms; one question; friendly tone.

## Exercise 10 — Habit or Happening Now?

> **LT pagalba:** Present Simple vartokite įpročiams ir faktams, Present Continuous—tam, kas vyksta dabar arba laikinai. Būsenos veiksmažodžiai, pvz., *know, like, need*, dažniausiai nevartojami Continuous formoje. Klaidos kodas: **T**.

2. Today I ___ (use) a desk at the library.
3. My sister ___ (not drink) coffee.
4. Why ___ you ___ (wear) a coat indoors?
5. The train often ___ (arrive) five minutes late.
6. Be quiet—the baby ___ (sleep).
7. We ___ (have) lunch now.
8. This soup ___ (taste) delicious.

### B. Find and correct the mistake. / Raskite ir ištaisykite po vieną klaidą.

1. I am knowing the answer.
2. She work in a hotel.
3. Are you usually taking the bus?
4. He is cook dinner at the moment.

### C. Describe two pictures without pictures

First describe your usual evening. Then imagine what your household is doing right now. / Pirmiausia apibūdinkite įprastą vakarą, tada—ką jūsų namiškiai veikia dabar.

**Model:** *I usually cook at six and eat with my family. After dinner, I read or watch a series. Right now, I am studying English. My partner is making tea, and our daughter is drawing at the table.*  
**Checklist:** two habitual actions; three actions now; time markers; correct auxiliary verbs.

## Exercise 11 — What Was Happening?

> **LT pagalba:** Past Continuous parodo vykusį foninį veiksmą, Past Simple—trumpesnį įvykį, kuris jį nutraukė. Dažnas modelis: *I was walking when it started to rain.* Klaidos kodas: **T**.

### A. Complete with Past Simple or Past Continuous. / Pasirinkite Past Simple arba Past Continuous.

1. I ___ (walk) home when it ___ (start) to snow.
2. While Tomas ___ (cook), the lights ___ (go) out.
3. We ___ (wait) for the bus when we ___ (see) the accident.
4. She ___ (drop) her cup while she ___ (talk) on the phone.
5. What ___ you ___ (do) at eight last night?
6. They ___ (not sleep) when I ___ (call).

### B. Join with **when** or **while**. / Sujunkite sakinius žodžiu **when** arba **while**.

1. I was checking my ticket. The train arrived.
2. Eva was cycling. She found a wallet.
3. We were eating dinner. Someone knocked at the door.

### C. Make a six-frame story

Describe the background, the interruption, what happened next, and the result.

**Model:** *It was raining, and I was driving home. While I was waiting at a red light, I saw a small dog in the road. I stopped the car and called the number on its collar. Ten minutes later, the owner arrived. She was very grateful.*  
**Checklist:** two background actions; one interrupting event; **when/while**; clear result.

## Exercise 13 — Objects at the Lost-Property Desk

> **LT pagalba:** Prieš daiktavardį vartokite *my/your/her*, o savarankiškai—*mine/yours/hers*. **This/that** yra vienaskaita, **these/those**—daugiskaita. Klaidos kodas: **DET**.

### A. Choose the correct determiner. / Pasirinkite tinkamą pažymimąjį arba savybinį žodį.

1. Is this **your / yours** umbrella?
2. That blue backpack is **my / mine**.
3. I found **a / an** earphone under the chair.
4. **These / This** gloves are too small for me.
5. Are **those / that** keys on the counter yours?
6. There are **some / any** coins in the pocket.
7. There aren't **some / any** names inside the bag.
8. **Each / Every** key has a different label.

### B. Identify the object without naming it.

Match each description to **charger, receipt, wallet, headphones**. / Susiekite aprašymą su tinkamu daiktu.

1. You use these to listen privately.
2. This shows what you bought and paid.
3. You keep cards and money in this.
4. This gives power to your phone.

### C. Lost-property role-play

Describe a lost object: colour, material, size, contents, and where you last saw it.

**Model:** *I've lost my small black wallet. It is made of leather and has two bank cards inside. I last saw it on the number 3 bus this morning. Has anyone brought it in?*  
**Checklist:** five identifying details; possessive form; polite question; no unnecessary private card details.

## Exercise 14 — People and Pronouns

> **LT pagalba:** *I/he/she/we/they* eina veiksnio vietoje; *me/him/her/us/them*—papildinio vietoje. Savybiniai *mine/hers/ours* pakeičia visą daiktavardinę frazę. Klaidos kodai: **WO**, **DET**.

### A. Replace the repeated words with suitable pronouns. / Pasikartojančius žodžius pakeiskite tinkamais įvardžiais.

1. I called Marta, but Marta didn't answer.
2. My parents gave my brother and me a plant.
3. That coat belongs to Darius.
4. Ema made the cake without help.
5. We enjoyed the party very much.
6. These seats belong to us.

### B. Choose the correct form. / Pasirinkite tinkamą įvardžio formą.

1. Could you help **I / me**?
2. We made the decorations **ourselves / us**.
3. Is this notebook **her / hers**?
4. They invited **we / us** to dinner.
5. My neighbour introduced **himself / him**.
6. I sent **she / her** a message.

### C. Introduce an important person

**Model:** *My cousin Milda is very important to me. I have known her all my life. She is patient and funny. We often help each other, and we can talk about anything. Last year, she taught me how to bake bread.*  
**Checklist:** relationship clear; subject and object pronouns; one reciprocal phrase such as **each other**; five sentences.

## Exercise 15 — Sharing Responsibilities at Home

> **LT pagalba:** **Have to** reiškia pareigą; **don't have to**—kad veiksmas nebūtinas. Su *he/she/it* vartokite **has to**, o neiginyje **doesn't have to**. Klaidos kodas: **VF**.

### A. Complete with **have to, has to, don't have to,** or **doesn't have to**. / Įrašykite pareigą arba pareigos nebuvimą reiškiančią formą.

1. I ___ take the bins out tonight; it is my turn.
2. Karolis ___ wash the dishes because the dishwasher is working.
3. We ___ be quiet after ten; it is a building rule.
4. My daughter ___ cook, but she likes helping.
5. ___ you ___ clean the stairs every week?
6. ___ your landlord ___ repair the broken window?

### B. Choose the household verb. / Pasirinkite su namų ruoša natūraliai vartojamą veiksmažodį.

1. ___ the bed: **make / do**
2. ___ the laundry: **do / take**
3. ___ the floor: **vacuum / put**
4. ___ the table before dinner: **set / wash**
5. ___ away the groceries: **put / make**
6. ___ the plants: **water / clean**

### C. Hold a two-minute house meeting

Agree on four jobs and one flexible arrangement.

**Model:** *I can cook on Monday and Wednesday. Could you do the washing-up on those days? We both have to keep the kitchen tidy. You don't have to vacuum this week because I did it yesterday. If we're busy on Friday, we can clean on Saturday instead.*  
**Checklist:** four jobs assigned; polite request; obligation and lack of obligation; fair backup plan.

## Exercise 42 — Thirty-Second Decisions

## Exercise 43 — Conversation Repair

> **LT pagalba:** Jei nesupratote, pokalbio nereikia nutraukti. Mandagiai paprašykite pakartoti, paaiškinti žodį, patvirtinti informaciją arba duoti laiko patikrinti.

Choose a helpful repair phrase. / Pasirinkite frazę, kuri padeda pataisyti ar tęsti pokalbį.

1. You did not hear: **Could you say that again, please? / Speak.**
2. You do not know a word: **What does “refund” mean? / Refund what?**
3. You need confirmation: **Do you mean next Thursday? / Thursday yes no?**
4. You made a mistake: **Sorry, let me correct that. / No, forget.**
5. You need time: **Could you give me a moment to check? / Wait.**

Practise each phrase with a calm voice. / Kiekvieną frazę ramiai pasakykite garsiai.

### Lithuanian repair map / Klaidų taisymo žemėlapis

| Exercise | If this was difficult... / Jei buvo sunku... | Lithuanian repair note / Taisymo paaiškinimas |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **am/is/are**, questions | **Am** vartojamas tik su *I*; klausime *am/is/are* keliamas prieš asmenį. |
| 2 | Present Simple | Su *he/she/it* teiginyje pridėkite **-s**, bet po *does/doesn't* vartokite bazinę formą. |
| 3 | Past Simple | Baigtas praeities laikas reikalauja Past Simple; netaisyklingų formų negalima sudaryti su **-ed**. |
| 4 | quantity words | **Many/few**—su suskaičiuojamais; **much/little**—su nesuskaičiuojamais daiktavardžiais. |
| 5 | directions | Patikrinkite ne tik prielinksnį, bet ir žvilgsnio tašką: *across from*—kitoje pusėje, *next to*—šalia. |
| 6 | future forms | Kalendoriaus susitarimas, ketinimas ir spontaniškas sprendimas anglų kalboje dažnai reiškiami skirtingomis formomis. |
| 7 | Present Perfect | Su konkrečiu baigtu laiku vartokite Past Simple; su patirtimi ar laikotarpiu iki dabar—Present Perfect. |
| 8 | comparisons | Nerašykite dvigubo laipsnio: ne *more cheaper*, o **cheaper**. Po aukščiausiojo laipsnio dažnai reikia **the**. |
| 9 | modals | Po modalinio veiksmažodžio eina bazinė forma be **to**. **Mustn't** reiškia „negalima“, **don't have to**—„nebūtina“. |
| 10 | present tenses | Laiko žodis padeda, bet svarbiausia reikšmė: įprotis ar faktas → Simple; vyksta dabar ar laikinai → Continuous. |
| 11 | past tenses | Ilgesnis vykęs fonas → Past Continuous; jį nutraukęs trumpas įvykis → Past Simple. |
| 12 | mixed grammar | Taisykite po vieną sluoksnį: laikas, forma, žodžių tvarka, kiekis, prielinksnis, stilius. |
| 13 | determiners | *My/your* turi eiti prieš daiktavardį; *mine/yours* vartojami be daiktavardžio. |
| 14 | pronouns | Paklauskite: kas atlieka veiksmą? → *I/he/she*; ką veiksmas pasiekia? → *me/him/her*. |
| 15 | obligation | **Doesn't have to** jau turi *does*, todėl *have* lieka be **-s**. |
| 16 | health language | Trukmė: **for two days**; pradžios momentas: **since Monday**. Aiškiai prašykite profesionalaus patarimo. |
| 17 | collocations | Neteisingą junginį taisykite mokydamiesi visą porą: **heavy rain**, ne pavienį žodį *heavy*. |
| 18 | verb patterns | Po prielinksnio vartokite **-ing**; po *want/decide/need* dažnai vartojama **to + verb**. |
| 19 | technology | Rinkitės įprastą junginį: *sign in, attach a file, save work, update an app*. Pažodinis vertimas dažnai klaidina. |
| 20 | invitations | *Would like to join; how about meeting; let's meet.* Pasižymėkite visą modelį, ne tik veiksmažodį. |
| 21 | quantities and conditionals | Po **if** ateities sąlygoje paprastai nevartokite *will*: *If it rains, we will stay inside.* |
| 22 | complaints | Pataisykite ne tik gramatiką, bet ir toną: faktas + lūkestis + mandagus, konkretus prašymas. |
| 23 | relative clauses | Žmogus → **who**, daiktas → **which**, vieta → **where**. Apibūdinimas eina iškart po daiktavardžio. |
| 24 | project language | Jei kalba stringa, trumpinkite sakinius ir naudokite aiškią seką. Tikslas—suprantamai susitarti, ne parodyti sudėtingą gramatiką. |
| 25–44 | bonus drills | Patikrinkite užduoties **LT pagalba** langelį, tada sukurkite vieną asmeninį pavyzdį su pataisyta forma. |
