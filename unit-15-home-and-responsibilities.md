# Unit 15: Home and Responsibilities

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Name rooms, furniture, and common chores.
- Distinguish **have to, don't have to, mustn't, can**.
- Agree on fair house rules.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **have to** for a rule or practical necessity. **Don't have to** means something is optional. **Mustn't** means it is forbidden. **Can** can express permission. In the past, use **had to**.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> <strong>have to</strong> reiškia „reikia / privaloma“, <strong>don't have to</strong> – „nebūtina“, <strong>mustn't</strong> – „draudžiama“, o <strong>can</strong> gali reikšti leidimą. Praeityje vartokite <strong>had to</strong>.</p>

## Exercise 1: Match Chores and Places / Pratimas 1

1. do the washing-up 2. make the bed 3. take out the rubbish 4. vacuum the floor 5. do the laundry

a. bedroom; b. kitchen sink; c. washing machine; d. bin area; e. living-room carpet

## Exercise 2: Choose the Rule / Pratimas 2

1. You **mustn't / don't have to** smoke here; it is forbidden.
2. This is required: guests **have to / can** collect a key at reception.
3. You **don't have to / mustn't** bring towels; we provide them.
4. Residents **can / had to** use the garden until 9 p.m.
5. Yesterday we **had to / have to** repair the sink.
6. **Can / Mustn't** I use the washing machine?

## Exercise 3: Classify the Notices / Pratimas 3

Label each notice **required, optional, permitted, forbidden**.

1. Shoes must be removed. 2. You can leave bicycles here. 3. You don't have to clean before noon. 4. No loud music after 10 p.m.

## Exercise 4: Read the Flat Advert / Pratimas 4

> Room available in a quiet two-bedroom flat. You can use the kitchen, balcony, and bicycle store. You have to pay rent by the third day of each month. You don't have to buy cleaning products because we share the cost. Guests can stay for two nights, but they mustn't make noise after 10 p.m.

1. Which shared spaces can a tenant use?
2. When is rent due?
3. Who pays for cleaning products?
4. What two rules apply to guests?

## Exercise 5: Negotiate the Chores / Pratimas 5

Complete with **can, have to, don't have to, mustn't**.

A: We _____ clean before our guests arrive. B: I _____ vacuum, but I can't shop today. A: That's fine. You _____ shop; I can do it. B: Great. We _____ forget to take out the rubbish.

## Exercise 6: Make a Fair Home Plan / Pratimas 6

Write four rules and divide three chores. Model: **We have to keep the kitchen clean. We mustn't play loud music late. Everyone can use the balcony. I can vacuum, my flatmate can do the washing-up, and our other flatmate can take out the rubbish. We don't have to cook together.**
