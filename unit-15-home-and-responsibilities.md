# Unit 15: Home and Responsibilities

## Learning Objectives / Mokymosi tikslai

- Name rooms, furniture, and common chores.
- Distinguish **have to, don't have to, mustn't, can**.
- Agree on fair house rules.

### Your route through this unit / Kaip mokytis

Begin with meaning, not translation. For every rule, label it **required, optional, permitted,** or **forbidden**. Then choose the English form. Finish by negotiating a plan rather than only listing rules.

## Language Focus / Kalbos pagrindai

Use **have to** for a rule or practical necessity. **Don't have to** means something is optional. **Mustn't** means it is forbidden. **Can** can express permission. In the past, use **had to**.

<p class="lt-text" lang="lt"><strong>Lietuviška atrama:</strong> <strong>have to</strong> reiškia „reikia / privaloma“, <strong>don't have to</strong> – „nebūtina“, <strong>mustn't</strong> – „draudžiama“, o <strong>can</strong> gali reikšti leidimą. Praeityje vartokite <strong>had to</strong>.</p>

### Meaning first / Pirmiausia reikšmė

| Meaning | Form | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| required now | **have/has to** | We **have to** pay the rent today. |
| required in the past | **had to** | I **had to** call the landlord yesterday. |
| optional | **don't/doesn't have to** | You **don't have to** bring a towel. |
| forbidden | **mustn't** | Guests **mustn't** smoke inside. |
| permitted | **can** | Residents **can** use the garden. |

**Do not confuse:**
**You don't have to wash the cups** = you may wash them, but it is optional.
**You mustn't wash these cups** = do not wash them; it is forbidden.

### Home problems and negotiation / Namų problemos ir susitarimai

- **Rooms and areas:** hall, bedroom, living room, balcony, shared kitchen
- **Problems:** blocked sink, broken shelf, noisy neighbour, full bin
- **Negotiating:** *I can do that. Could you...? That's fair. How about...? Let's take turns.*

### Mini-dialogue: make the rule fair

> A: We have to clean before Friday. I can vacuum.
> B: Fine. Could you also take out the rubbish?
> A: I can do that if you wash the floor.
> B: That's fair. We don't have to do everything tonight.

Notice that a useful negotiation includes a need, an offer, a request, and an agreement.

### Chore collocations / Namų ruošos junginiai

| Chore | Natural chunk | Lithuanian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| washing-up | do the washing-up / load the dishwasher | plauti indus / sudėti indus į indaplovę |
| laundry | do the laundry / hang out the washing | skalbti / padžiauti skalbinius |
| rubbish | take out the rubbish / sort the recycling | išnešti šiukšles / rūšiuoti atliekas |
| surfaces | wipe the table / clean the worktop | nuvalyti stalą / stalviršį |
| floors | sweep, mop, or vacuum the floor | šluoti, plauti ar siurbti grindis |

**Have to** often reports an outside rule or practical need: *We have to pay rent by Friday.* **Must** can sound more direct and personal: *I must call the landlord.* In everyday questions, **Do I have to...?** is more common than **Must I...?**

### Sound Focus / Tarimo atrama

In quick speech, **have to** often sounds like **haf-ta**, but write **have to**. The final **-n't** carries important meaning in **don't have to** and **mustn't**, so do not swallow it. Practise the contrast: **You have to clean it. / You don't have to clean it. / You mustn't clean it.**

## Exercise 2: Choose the Rule / Pratimas 2

1. You **mustn't / don't have to** smoke here; it is forbidden.
2. This is required: guests **have to / can** collect a key at reception.
3. You **don't have to / mustn't** bring towels; we provide them.
4. Residents **can / had to** use the garden until 9 p.m.
5. Yesterday we **had to / have to** repair the sink.
6. **Can / Mustn't** I use the washing machine?

## Exercise 3: Classify the Notices / Pratimas 3

Label each notice **required, optional, permitted, forbidden**.

1. Shoes must be removed. 2. You can leave bicycles here. 3. You don't have to clean before noon. 4. No loud music after 10 p.m.

## Exercise 4: Read the Flat Advert / Pratimas 4

Before answering, make four columns: **space, money, supplies, guests**. Put each fact from the advert in one column.

> Room available in a quiet two-bedroom flat. You can use the kitchen, balcony, and bicycle store. You have to pay rent by the third day of each month. You don't have to buy cleaning products because we share the cost. Guests can stay for two nights, but they mustn't make noise after 10 p.m.

1. Which shared spaces can a tenant use?
2. When is rent due?
3. Who pays for cleaning products?
4. What two rules apply to guests?

## Exercise 5: Negotiate the Chores / Pratimas 5

Complete with **can, have to, don't have to, mustn't**.

A: We _____ clean before our guests arrive. B: I _____ vacuum, but I can't shop today. A: That's fine. You _____ shop; I can do it. B: Great. We _____ forget to take out the rubbish.

## Exercise 6: Make a Fair Home Plan / Pratimas 6

Write four rules and divide three chores. Model: **We have to keep the kitchen clean. We mustn't play loud music late. Everyone can use the balcony. I can vacuum, my flatmate can do the washing-up, and our other flatmate can take out the rubbish. We don't have to cook together.**

**Sentence starters:**
**Everyone has to... / Nobody can... / We mustn't... / We don't have to... / I can..., while... / Let's take turns...**

**Fair-plan checklist:**

- [ ] My plan includes something required, optional, permitted, and forbidden.
- [ ] Three chores have a named person or a turn-taking system.
- [ ] I included one polite suggestion or request.
- [ ] The rules are realistic and do not contradict one another.

### Two-person rehearsal / Dialogo repeticija

Role A wants a very quiet, tidy home. Role B works late and has little time on weekdays. Negotiate three rules. If you study alone, record both roles, leaving a two-second pause before each response.

Useful repair phrases: **Do you mean...? / I can't do that on Monday, but I can... / Could we change...? / So we agree that...**

## Exercise 7: Necessary, Optional, or Forbidden? / Pratimas 7

Complete with **have to, don't have to, mustn't, can**. Use each form more than once if necessary.

1. The lift is broken, so we _____ use the stairs today.
2. You _____ leave rubbish in the hall; it blocks the exit.
3. We provide bed linen, so guests _____ bring any.
4. Residents _____ book the shared room for small meetings.
5. I _____ phone the landlord because water is coming through the ceiling.
6. You _____ feed the neighbour's cat; she has already fed it.
7. Visitors _____ park in front of the emergency gate.
8. _____ I keep my bicycle in the store?

## Exercise 8: A Flatmate Meeting / Pratimas 8

Read the dialogue.

> **Nora:** The kitchen is often untidy. Can we make a simple plan?
> **Paulius:** Yes. We all have to wash our own dishes.
> **Nora:** Agreed. Do we have to clean the whole kitchen every day?
> **Paulius:** No, we don't have to do that. We can do a bigger clean on Saturday.
> **Nora:** I can mop the floor, but I can't carry the recycling downstairs.
> **Paulius:** I can do that. And we mustn't leave food out at night because of the cat.
> **Nora:** Good. I'll write the plan on the fridge.

1. What does everyone have to wash?
2. Do they have to clean the whole kitchen daily?
3. When can they do a bigger clean?
4. Which chore can Nora do?
5. What can Paulius do?
6. Why mustn't they leave food out?

## Exercise 10: Read, Cover, and Remember the House Note / Pratimas 10

Ask a partner to read the note twice, or read it aloud yourself. Cover it before answering.

> **House note:** On Wednesday, a plumber is coming between nine and eleven. Someone has to be at home to open the door. You don't have to clear the whole bathroom, but please move towels and bottles away from the sink. The plumber can park beside the building. He mustn't use the space marked 12 because it belongs to our neighbour.

1. Who is coming?
2. On which day and at what time?
3. Why does someone have to stay home?
4. What do residents not have to clear?
5. What should they move?
6. Where can the visitor park, and where mustn't he park?

## Exercise 11: Agree on a Fair Chore Plan / Pratimas 11

Work with a partner, or speak both roles. You share a flat and need a one-week plan. Discuss dishes, rubbish, laundry, floors, quiet time, and one optional shared activity.

Useful language: **Can you...? / I can..., but I can't... / Do we have to...? / We don't have to... / We mustn't... / Is that fair?**

Model: **A: We have to clean before Friday. Can you vacuum? B: Yes, but I can't do it tonight. I can do it tomorrow. Do I have to mop too? A: No, you don't have to. I'll mop. We mustn't forget the rubbish.**

Success checklist:

- [ ] We divided at least three chores.
- [ ] We made one necessary, one optional, and one forbidden rule.
- [ ] We checked that the plan was fair.
- [ ] I did not confuse **don't have to** with **mustn't**.

## Can-Do Check / Gebėjimų įsivertinimas

- [ ] I can understand household rules.
- [ ] I can say what is required, optional, permitted, or forbidden.
- [ ] I can negotiate a fair division of responsibilities.
- [ ] I can negotiate chores and respond politely to a problem at home.
