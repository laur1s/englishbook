## Part 1: Essential Vocabulary / Pagrindinis žodynas

### Greetings (Pasisveikinimai)
- Hello / Hi - Labas, Sveiki
- Good morning - Labas rytas
- Good afternoon - Laba diena
- Good evening - Labas vakaras
- Good night - Labanakt
- Goodbye / Bye - Viso gero / Iki
- See you later - Iki pasimatymo

### Introductions (Prisistatymai)
- My name is... - Mano vardas...
- I am... - Aš esu...
- This is... - Tai yra...
- Nice to meet you - Malonu susipažinti
- How are you? - Kaip laikaisi?
- I'm fine, thank you - Aš gerai, ačiū
- And you? - O tu?

---

## Part 2: Grammar - Verb "To Be" / Gramatika - Veiksmažodis "Būti"

### Present Simple of "To Be"

| Subject (Veiksnys) | Verb | Lithuanian |
|-------------------|------|------------|
| I | am | aš esu |
| You | are | tu esi |
| He/She/It | is | jis/ji yra |
| We | are | mes esame |
| You (plural) | are | jūs esate |
| They | are | jie/jos yra |

### Contractions (Sutrumpinimai)
- I am = I'm
- You are = You're
- He is = He's
- She is = She's
- We are = We're
- They are = They're

### Negative Form (Neigiamoji forma)
- I am not = I'm not
- You are not = You aren't / You're not
- He is not = He isn't / He's not

## Exercise 4: Questions with "to be"

### Question form (Klausimo forma)
- Am I...? - Ar aš esu...?
- Are you...? - Ar tu esi...?
- Is he/she/it...? - Ar jis/ji yra...?
- Are we...? - Ar mes esame...?

## Useful Phrases / Naudingi posakiai

- What's your name? - Koks tavo vardas?
- Where are you from? - Iš kur tu esi?
- How old are you? - Kiek tau metų?
- What do you do? - Kuo tu užsiimi?
- Pleased to meet you - Malonu susipažinti
- How do you spell that? - Kaip tai rašoma?
- Can you repeat that? - Ar gali pakartoti?
- I don't understand - Aš nesuprantu

---

## Cultural Notes / Kultūrinės pastabos

In English-speaking countries:
- People shake hands when meeting for the first time
- "How are you?" is often a greeting, not always a real question
- First names are used more commonly than in Lithuania
- Eye contact is important during greetings
