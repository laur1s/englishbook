# A2.2 Follow-Up Mini-Course: Five Real-Life Sessions
## Trumpas A2.2 įtvirtinimo kursas: penkios praktinės pamokos

This optional mini-course is for learners who have completed the A2.2 checkpoint in Unit 12 and want a short, practical review before Unit 13. It does not introduce a new official level: **A2.2 is an internal stage of this A2 course**. / Šis pasirenkamas mini kursas skirtas baigusiems 12 skyriaus A2.2 tarpinį patikrinimą ir norintiems trumpai įtvirtinti žinias prieš 13 skyrių. Tai nėra naujas oficialus kalbos lygis: **A2.2 yra vidinis šio A2 kurso etapas**.

The five sessions follow one real-life situation: choosing and joining a community activity. Each session takes about **20–25 minutes** and ends with English you can use outside an exercise. / Penkios pamokos siejamos su viena gyvenimiška situacija – bendruomenės veiklos pasirinkimu ir lankymu. Vienai pamokai skirkite maždaug **20–25 minučių**. Kiekvienos pamokos pabaigoje pavartosite anglų kalbą praktiškai.

**Course result:** five corrected grammar sentences, one activity recommendation, one short incident story, one 50–70-word final message, and one 60-second recording. / **Kurso rezultatas:** penki pataisyti gramatikos sakiniai, viena veiklos rekomendacija, trumpa įvykio istorija, 50–70 žodžių galutinė žinutė ir 60 sekundžių įrašas.

## How to use the course / Mokymosi tvarka

Use one session per day, or leave a day between Sessions 3 and 4. Do the fixed exercise before opening the answer key. Open tasks are not automatically graded and do not receive points; use the model and checklist to review your own work. / Atlikite po vieną pamoką per dieną arba tarp 3 ir 4 pamokų padarykite dienos pertrauką. Uždarą pratimą atlikite prieš atverdami atsakymus. Atviros užduotys automatiškai nevertinamos ir už jas neskiriami taškai; savo darbą tikrinkite pagal pavyzdį ir kontrolinį sąrašą.

For every session:

1. **Recall – 3 min.** Say two sentences without looking at your notes. / **Prisiminkite – 3 min.** Pasakykite du sakinius nežiūrėdami į užrašus.
2. **Notice – 4 min.** Read the rule and model aloud. / **Pastebėkite – 4 min.** Perskaitykite taisyklę ir pavyzdį garsiai.
3. **Check – 6 min.** Complete the fixed-answer exercise. / **Pasitikrinkite – 6 min.** Atlikite uždarą pratimą.
4. **Use – 7–10 min.** Write or say your own answer. / **Pavartokite – 7–10 min.** Parašykite arba pasakykite savo atsakymą.
5. **Repair – 2 min.** Choose one mistake, write the corrected sentence, and make a new example. / **Ištaisykite – 2 min.** Išsirinkite vieną klaidą, parašykite taisyklingą sakinį ir sukurkite naują pavyzdį.

Keep the answer key closed until the fixed task is complete. If an answer differs from the key but has the same accurate meaning, compare the context before changing it. / Neatverkite atsakymų, kol neatlikote uždaros užduoties. Jei jūsų atsakymas skiriasi, bet yra taisyklingas ir perteikia tą pačią prasmę, prieš taisydami dar kartą įvertinkite kontekstą.

## Course map / Mokymosi planas

1. **Present Perfect + Past Simple:** an experience interview with a finished detail. / Pokalbis apie patirtį ir konkrečią praeities detalę.
2. **Comparisons and modal meaning:** an activity recommendation with clear reasons. / Veiklos rekomendacija su aiškiomis priežastimis.
3. **Present-tense choice:** a five-sentence progress update. / Penkių sakinių pažangos žinutė.
4. **Past Continuous + Past Simple:** a six-sentence incident story. / Šešių sakinių istorija apie netikėtą įvykį.
5. **Mixed A2.2 communication:** a 50–70-word reply and a 60-second recording. / 50–70 žodžių atsakymas ir 60 sekundžių įrašas.

This course reviews [Unit 7](/a2/unit-07), [Unit 8](/a2/unit-08), [Unit 9](/a2/unit-09), [Unit 10](/a2/unit-10), [Unit 11](/a2/unit-11), and the [Unit 12 checkpoint](/a2/unit-12). If a form feels completely new, pause and reopen the relevant full unit instead of guessing. / Kursas kartoja 7–12 skyrių medžiagą. Jei forma atrodo visiškai nauja, trumpam sustokite ir atsiverskite atitinkamą pilną pamoką.

## Session 1 — Experience and the finished story / Patirtis ir baigtas praeities įvykis

**Goal:** Ask about life experience with the Present Perfect, then use the Past Simple for a finished detail. / **Tikslas:** paklausti apie patirtį ir pateikti konkrečią baigtos praeities detalę.

Use the **Present Perfect** when the experience is connected to now and no finished past time is given: *Have you ever taken an evening course?* Use the **Past Simple** for a finished past event or a specific past detail, especially with a finished-time phrase: *What course did you take? I took it last autumn.* / Apie gyvenimo patirtį be konkretaus baigto laiko vartokite **Present Perfect**. Kalbėdami apie baigtą praeities įvykį ar konkrečią praeities detalę, ypač su baigto laiko posakiu, vartokite **Past Simple**.

**Model dialogue:** / Pavyzdinis dialogas:

> **A:** Have you ever taken an evening course?  
> **B:** Yes, I have.  
> **A:** What course did you take?  
> **B:** I took a photography course last autumn. I really enjoyed it.

### Exercise 1 — Complete the experience interview / Užpildykite pokalbį apie patirtį

Complete each sentence with the Present Perfect or Past Simple form of the verb. The time words make the intended answer clear. / Įrašykite Present Perfect arba Past Simple formą. Laiko žodžiai padeda aiškiai pasirinkti.

1. ___ you ever ___ an evening course? *(take)*
2. Yes, I ___. I ___ a first-aid course last winter. *(take)*
3. Greta ___ never ___ a cooking class. *(try)*
4. We ___ the community centre last Tuesday. *(visit)*
5. Tomas ___ to the tutor twice this week, and the week is not over. *(speak)*
6. When ___ you ___ the photography group? *(join)*

### Use it — Run a three-question interview / Surenkite trijų klausimų interviu

Ask a partner three **Have you ever...?** questions about courses, hobbies, travel, food, or events. After every *yes*, ask for one finished detail in the Past Simple. If you are studying alone, record both roles. / Užduokite partneriui tris **Have you ever...?** klausimus. Po kiekvieno teigiamo atsakymo paklauskite vienos konkrečios praeities detalės Past Simple laiku. Jei mokotės vieni, įrašykite abu vaidmenis.

**Starters:** / Sakinių pradžios:

- *Have you ever joined...?*
- *When did you...?*
- *Where did you...?*
- *What was it like?*

**Model:** / Pavyzdys:

*Have you ever joined a sports club? Yes, I have. When did you join it? I joined a running club two years ago, but I only stayed for one month.*

**Checklist:** / Kontrolinis sąrašas:

Three experience questions; at least two Past Simple follow-ups; one clear finished-time phrase; complete answers rather than only *yes* or *no*.

**Repair line:** / Taisymo sakinys:

If you wrote *I have joined it last year*, change it to *I joined it last year*, then make one new sentence with *yesterday, last...,* or *in 2025*.

## Session 2 — Compare, understand the rule, and choose / Palyginkite, supraskite taisyklę ir pasirinkite

**Goal:** Compare factual options and choose a modal verb by meaning. / **Tikslas:** palyginti faktus ir parinkti modalinį veiksmažodį pagal prasmę.

Three activities are available at the Riverside Community Centre:

- **Photography Walk:** Tuesday at 18:00; €24 per month; 8 learners; outdoors; bring a phone or camera.
- **Cooking Club:** Thursday at 18:30; €30 per month; 12 learners; kitchen; ingredients included.
- **Conversation Club:** Saturday at 11:00; €12 per month; 10 learners; library; no equipment needed.

Use comparisons only for the facts shown above. Your final choice can be personal, so more than one recommendation can be valid when the reasons are accurate. / Lyginkite tik pateiktus faktus. Galutinis pasirinkimas gali būti asmeninis, todėl keli atsakymai gali būti tinkami, jei priežastys tikslios.

Remember the meanings:

- **should** – advice / patarimas;
- **might** – possibility / galimybė;
- **mustn't** – something is forbidden / draudžiama;
- **don't have to** – something is not necessary / nebūtina.

After **should, might,** and **mustn't**, use the base verb without **to**: *should try, might move, mustn't enter*. In *don't have to bring*, **to** is part of **have to**. / Po **should, might** ir **mustn't** vartokite bendratį be **to**. Junginyje *don't have to bring* žodis **to** yra konstrukcijos **have to** dalis.

### Exercise 2 — Complete the comparison and modal choices / Užbaikite palyginimus ir parinkite modalus

Complete 1–6 with the correct adjective form. Use **should, might, mustn't,** and **don't have to** once each in 7–10. / 1–6 sakiniuose įrašykite tinkamą būdvardžio formą. 7–10 sakiniuose po vieną kartą pavartokite nurodytus modalus.

1. Conversation Club is the ___ option. *(cheap)*
2. Photography Walk has a ___ group than Conversation Club. *(small)*
3. Cooking Club is ___ than Photography Walk. *(expensive)*
4. Cooking Club has the ___ group. *(large)*
5. Cooking Club starts ___ in the evening than Photography Walk. *(late)*
6. Conversation Club is not as ___ as Photography Walk. *(expensive)*
7. Dark clouds are coming, so Photography Walk ___ move inside.
8. The kitchen sign says “Do not enter before the tutor arrives.” Visitors ___ enter early.
9. The Cooking Club price includes all ingredients, so learners ___ bring food.
10. Your friend wants to speak more English. She ___ try Conversation Club.

### Use it — Recommend one activity / Rekomenduokite vieną veiklą

Choose an activity for yourself or a friend. Give two factual comparisons and explain one possible difficulty. Different choices are valid. / Pasirinkite veiklą sau arba draugui. Pateikite du faktais pagrįstus palyginimus ir įvardykite vieną galimą sunkumą. Gali tikti skirtingi pasirinkimai.

**Starter:** / Sakinio pradžia:

*I would choose... It is... than... I should... It might...*

**Model:** / Pavyzdys:

*I would choose Conversation Club. It is the cheapest option, and its group is smaller than the Cooking Club group. I want to speak more, so I should practise there. I might feel nervous at first, but I can prepare two questions.*

**Checklist:** / Kontrolinis sąrašas:

One clear choice; two accurate comparisons; one modal with the intended meaning; one personal reason; four or five connected sentences.

**Repair line:** / Taisymo sakinys:

Check for double comparisons such as *more cheaper*. Use either *cheaper* or *more expensive*, not both comparison markers together.

## Session 3 — Usual, now, and so far / Įprastai, dabar ir iki šiol

**Goal:** Choose a present tense from the meaning and time context. / **Tikslas:** pasirinkti esamąjį laiką pagal prasmę ir laiko kontekstą.

Milda joined Conversation Club three weeks ago. The club also meets online. Use:

- **Present Simple** for a routine or state: *She usually attends on Saturday. She knows six members.*
- **Present Continuous** for an action happening now or a temporary situation: *Today she is attending online from home.*
- **Present Perfect** for progress up to now: *She has attended three meetings so far.*
- **Past Simple** for a finished past time: *She gave a talk last Saturday.*

Verbs that describe states, such as **know, need,** and **like**, are usually used in simple forms when they describe a state. This is a useful tendency, not a rule for every possible context. / Būseną nusakantys veiksmažodžiai, pvz., **know, need, like**, būsenos reikšme dažniausiai vartojami paprastosiomis formomis. Tai naudinga tendencija, o ne visiems kontekstams tinkanti absoliuti taisyklė.

### Exercise 3 — Complete Milda's update / Užpildykite Mildos pažangos žinutę

Use the correct form of the verb. / Įrašykite tinkamą veiksmažodžio formą.

1. Milda usually ___ Conversation Club on Saturday. *(attend)*
2. Today she ___ online from home because she has a cold. *(attend)*
3. She ___ six members now. *(know)*
4. She ___ three meetings so far. *(attend)*
5. She ___ any meetings since she joined. *(not miss)*
6. Last Saturday she ___ a short talk about her town. *(give)*
7. Right now, she ___ new vocabulary in her notebook. *(write)*

### Use it — Send a five-sentence progress update / Parašykite penkių sakinių pažangos žinutę

Write about a real or imagined class, hobby, or study routine. Include a routine, an action now, progress so far, a state, and one finished past action. / Parašykite apie tikrą arba išgalvotą pamoką, pomėgį ar mokymosi rutiną. Paminėkite įprotį, veiksmą dabar, pažangą iki šiol, būseną ir vieną baigtą praeities veiksmą.

**Starters:** / Sakinių pradžios:

*I usually... Today I am... So far, I have... I know/like/need... Last week, I...*

**Model:** / Pavyzdys:

*I usually go to Conversation Club on Saturdays. Today I am practising at home. So far, I have completed three dialogues. I know two people in the group now. Last week, I told a short story about my holiday.*

**Checklist:** / Kontrolinis sąrašas:

Five sentences; five clear time contexts; one suitable form in each sentence; no Continuous form with *know* in the state meaning; understandable real-life message.

**Repair line:** / Taisymo sakinys:

Underline the time phrase in each sentence. If there is no time phrase, write a one-word meaning label: **routine, now, progress, state,** or **finished past**.

## Session 4 — Background, event, and result / Fonas, įvykis ir rezultatas

**Goal:** Set a past scene with the Past Continuous and move the story forward with the Past Simple. / **Tikslas:** apibūdinti praeities foną ir papasakoti pagrindinius įvykius.

The Past Continuous often shows an action in progress around a past moment. The Past Simple often shows a completed event in the story. Choose from the meaning; **when** and **while** can help, but they do not choose the tense by themselves. / Past Continuous dažnai nusako tam tikru praeities momentu vykusį veiksmą, o Past Simple – baigtą istorijos įvykį. Laiką rinkitės pagal prasmę. **When** ir **while** gali padėti, bet vieni patys laiko nenulemia.

### Exercise 4 — Complete the community-centre incident / Užpildykite istoriją apie įvykį bendruomenės centre

Use the Past Continuous or Past Simple. / Vartokite Past Continuous arba Past Simple.

At 18:20, the club members ___ the room. *(prepare)* Aistė ___ cups while Benas ___ chairs. *(carry, move)* When Aistė ___ the kitchen door, the lights ___ out. *(open, go)* Everyone ___. *(stop)* The tutor ___ on her phone light. At that moment, the caretaker ___ the light switches. *(turn, check)* After five minutes, the lights ___ back on. *(come)*

### Use it — Tell a six-sentence incident story / Papasakokite šešių sakinių istoriją

Tell a safe, everyday story about something that happened during a journey, class, meal, walk, or day at home. You may invent it. / Papasakokite saugią kasdienišką istoriją apie įvykį kelionėje, pamokoje, valgant, vaikštant ar būnant namuose. Istoriją galite sugalvoti.

**Starter:** / Sakinio pradžia:

*Yesterday at..., I was... while... When..., ... In the end,...*

**Model:** / Pavyzdys:

*Yesterday I was walking to the centre while my friend was talking on the phone. We were crossing the road when a bag fell from her bicycle. Some papers fell onto the road. A driver stopped and waited. I picked up the papers, and my friend thanked the driver. In the end, nothing was broken.*

**Checklist:** / Kontrolinis sąrašas:

Exactly six sentences; two background actions; one clear main event; *when* or *while* used meaningfully; a result or ending; verbs understandable when read aloud.

**Repair line:** / Taisymo sakinys:

Draw one line under the background actions and two lines under the completed events. Then read the story again and check **was/were + -ing**.

## Session 5 — Community open-day challenge / Bendruomenės atvirų durų dienos užduotis

**Goal:** Understand practical information and write one connected A2.2 reply. / **Tikslas:** suprasti praktinę informaciją ir parašyti rišlų A2.2 lygio atsakymą.

### Exercise 5 — Read the notice and Ieva's message / Perskaitykite skelbimą ir Ievos žinutę

**Riverside Community Open Day — Saturday**

- **10:30 — Short conversation class:** free; Room 2; 12 places.
- **12:00 — Photo walk:** €5; 45 minutes; meet at the front desk.
- **13:30 — Cooking class:** €8; kitchen; 10 places.

Register online **before Friday at 18:00**. If it rains, the photo walk will meet in the main hall instead.

> **Ieva:** I've never taken part in an activity at this centre. I usually work on Saturdays, but this Saturday I'm free. Last year, I was walking through the Old Town when I saw a photo group. It looked fun. I want the cheapest paid activity, but I don't want to cook. The weather forecast says rain. What should I choose? Is anything free?

Answer from the notice and message. / Atsakykite remdamiesi skelbimu ir žinute.

1. When must people register?
2. Which activity is free?
3. Which is the cheapest paid activity?
4. Where will the photo walk meet if it rains?
5. Has Ieva taken part in an activity at this centre before?
6. What was Ieva doing when she saw a photo group last year?
7. Which activity matches all her stated conditions, and why?

### Final task — Reply, record, and compare / Atsakykite, įrašykite ir palyginkite

Write a **50–70-word** reply to Ieva. Then read it aloud or use it as notes for a **60-second** recording. Do not give yourself points; compare the meaning and requested language with the model and checklist. / Parašykite Ievai **50–70 žodžių** atsakymą. Tada jį perskaitykite garsiai arba naudokite kaip pastabas **60 sekundžių** įrašui. Neskirkite sau taškų; prasmę ir pavartotas formas palyginkite su pavyzdžiu bei kontroliniu sąrašu.

Include:

- one clear recommendation;
- one factual comparison;
- **should** for advice;
- **might** or **could** for a possibility or option;
- one Present Perfect sentence connected to now;
- a friendly ending.

**Starter:** / Sakinio pradžia:

*Hi Ieva, I think you should... It is... than... You haven't... It might...*

**Model:** / Pavyzdys:

*Hi Ieva, I think you should choose the photo walk. It is cheaper than the cooking class, and it matches your interest in photography. You haven't tried an activity here before, so the open day is a good first step. If it rains, the group will meet in the main hall. The short conversation class is free, so you could try that first too.*

**Checklist:** / Kontrolinis sąrašas:

50–70 words; all six requested parts; modal + base verb; no finished-time phrase with the Present Perfect; message answers Ieva's real questions; speech is clear enough to understand without reading the text.

## Final evidence and self-check / Galutinis darbas ir įsivertinimas

Keep these four pieces of evidence: / Išsaugokite šiuos keturis pažangos įrodymus:

1. one corrected sentence from each fixed exercise;
2. your Session 2 recommendation;
3. your six-sentence incident story;
4. your final message and 60-second recording.

Choose **Not yet / Almost / Yes** for each statement, then point to one piece of evidence. / Prie kiekvieno teiginio pasirinkite **Dar ne / Beveik / Taip** ir nurodykite vieną tai patvirtinantį darbą.

- I can ask about experience and add a finished past detail.
- I can compare factual options and explain a personal choice.
- I can separate advice, possibility, prohibition, and lack of necessity.
- I can describe a routine, an action now, and progress so far.
- I can set a past scene and tell what happened next.
- I can write and say a short connected reply to a real-life question.

If two or more statements are **Not yet**, repeat only the related session or sessions after two days. You do not need to restart the whole mini-course. / Jei prie dviejų ar daugiau teiginių pažymėjote **Dar ne**, po dviejų dienų pakartokite tik susijusią pamoką ar pamokas. Viso mini kurso pradėti iš naujo nereikia.

## Answer Key / Atsakymų raktas

### Exercise 1

1. **Have** you ever **taken**  
2. **have; took**  
3. **has** never **tried**  
4. **visited**  
5. **has spoken**  
6. **did** you **join**

**Repair note:** / Taisymo pastaba:

*Ever, never,* and an unfinished *this week* support the Present Perfect here. *Last winter* and *last Tuesday* give finished past times; the *when* question asks about a specific finished event.

### Exercise 2

1. **cheapest**  
2. **smaller**  
3. **more expensive**  
4. **largest**  
5. **later**  
6. **expensive**  
7. **might**  
8. **mustn't**  
9. **don't have to**  
10. **should**

**Repair note:** / Taisymo pastaba:

The activity details supply the comparison facts. In 8, entry is forbidden; in 9, bringing food is only unnecessary. **Mustn't** and **don't have to** do not mean the same thing.

### Exercise 3

1. **attends**  
2. **is attending**  
3. **knows**  
4. **has attended**  
5. **hasn't missed**  
6. **gave**  
7. **is writing**

**Repair note:** / Taisymo pastaba:

*Usually* marks a routine, *today/right now* marks the current temporary action, *so far/since she joined* connects progress to now, and *last Saturday* is finished past time.

### Exercise 4

**were preparing; was carrying; was moving; opened; went; stopped; turned; was checking; came**

Full text: *At 18:20, the club members **were preparing** the room. Aistė **was carrying** cups while Benas **was moving** chairs. When Aistė **opened** the kitchen door, the lights **went** out. Everyone **stopped**. The tutor **turned** on her phone light. At that moment, the caretaker **was checking** the light switches. After five minutes, the lights **came** back on.*

**Repair note:** / Taisymo pastaba:

The longer scene actions are in progress; *opened, went, stopped, turned,* and *came* move this particular story forward. The meaning of the event is more important than memorising a mechanical rule for *when* and *while*.

### Exercise 5

1. **Before Friday at 18:00.**  
2. **The short conversation class.**  
3. **The Photo walk (€5).**  
4. **In the main hall.**  
5. **No. She has never taken part in an activity there.**  
6. **She was walking through the Old Town.**  
7. **The Photo walk**, because she is free that Saturday, it is the cheapest paid activity, she does not want to cook, and the notice gives a rainy-weather meeting place.

For the open tasks, answers vary. Use the model beside each task and its checklist; do not treat the model as the only valid response. / Atvirų užduočių atsakymai gali skirtis. Remkitės prie užduoties pateiktu pavyzdžiu ir kontroliniu sąrašu; pavyzdys nėra vienintelis galimas atsakymas.
