## Part 2: Grammar - Past Simple / Gramatika

### Regular Verbs (Taisyklingi veiksmažodžiai)

**Formation:** verb + **-ed**

| Infinitive | Past Simple | Lithuanian |
|------------|-------------|------------|
| work | worked | dirbo |
| play | played | žaidė |
| watch | watched | žiūrėjo |
| start | started | pradėjo |
| finish | finished | baigė |
| live | lived | gyveno |
| study | studied | studijavo |
| try | tried | bandė |
| stop | stopped | sustojo |

### Spelling Rules (Rašybos taisyklės)

1. Most verbs: add **-ed** → work + ed = worked
2. Verbs ending in -e: add **-d** → live + d = lived
3. Verbs ending in consonant + y: **y → ied** → study → studied
4. Verbs ending in one vowel + one consonant: **double consonant + ed** → stop → stopped

## Part 3: Negative and Questions / Neigiamoji forma ir klausimai

### Negative (Neigiamasis)
**didn't (did not) + infinitive**

- I **didn't work** yesterday. - Aš nedirbau vakar.
- She **didn't go** to school. - Ji nenuėjo į mokyklą.
- They **didn't have** breakfast. - Jie nepusryčiavo.

⚠️ **Important:** After didn't, use the base form (infinitive) of the verb!
- I didn't worked ✗ → I didn't work ✓

### Questions (Klausimai)
**Did + subject + infinitive**

- **Did** you **work** yesterday? - Ar tu dirbai vakar?
- **Did** she **go** to school? - Ar ji nuėjo į mokyklą?
- **Did** they **have** breakfast? - Ar jie pusryčiavo?

**Short answers:**
- Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
- Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.

## Tips for Lithuanian Speakers / Patarimai lietuviams

⚠️ **Common mistakes:**
1. Don't forget irregular verbs - they don't add -ed!
2. Use "did" for questions and negatives, then base form of verb
3. Past Simple "was/were" doesn't use "did"
4. All subjects use the same past form (except was/were)

**Lithuanian vs English:**
- Lithuanian has more past tenses, English Past Simple is simpler
- Lithuanian verbs change more, English regular verbs just add -ed
